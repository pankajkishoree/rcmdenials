CO-119

CO-119 Denial Code: Not Covered Benefit / Benefit Exclusion

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Contractual Obligation

📋 What is CO-119 Denial Code?

CO-119 is a Contractual Obligation (CO) denial code indicating that the service is not a covered benefit under the patient's insurance plan. This means the patient's policy explicitly excludes or does not provide coverage for the specific service or procedure billed.

Unlike medical necessity denials, CO-119 indicates that even if the service was medically necessary, the patient's plan does not include it as a covered benefit. Understanding the patient's benefit plan details is essential before appealing this type of denial.

🔍 Root Causes of CO-119 Denials

📋

Plan Exclusions

  • Service explicitly excluded in member contract
  • Cosmetic or elective procedure exclusions
  • Experimental or investigational treatment
  • Weight loss surgery exclusion
  • Hearing, vision, or dental exclusions
🔒

Policy Limitations

  • Annual benefit maximum reached
  • Lifetime benefit limit exhausted
  • Service frequency limitation exceeded
  • Age-based coverage restrictions
  • Service setting limitations (outpatient vs. inpatient)
🔬

Experimental Treatment

  • Treatment not FDA approved for indication
  • Investigational drug or device
  • Off-label use without supporting literature
  • Clinical trial-related services
  • Procedure not recognized by specialty society
💊

Non-Formulary Items

  • Drug not on payer formulary
  • Brand-name drug when generic available
  • Non-preferred brand tier exclusion
  • Medical device not covered
  • Supply items excluded from outpatient benefit

✅ Step-by-Step Resolution Process

1

Review the Member's Benefit Plan

Obtain the patient's Summary Plan Description (SPD) or certificate of coverage. Identify the specific exclusion or limitation cited by the payer for the denied service.

💡 Pro Tip: Use the payer's online portal to view the member's specific benefit details. The exclusion is often listed in the "Exclusions and Limitations" section of the plan document.
2

Check for Benefit Exceptions

Some plans allow for benefit exceptions or medical necessity overrides even for excluded services. Review the plan's appeals policy for non-covered benefit exceptions.

3

Contact the Payer Pre-Appeal

Call the payer to discuss whether any exceptions, waivers, or alternative billing options are available. Ask if the service could be covered under a different benefit category.

4

File a Formal Appeal

If the service qualifies for a benefit exception, submit a formal appeal with supporting documentation including medical necessity evidence, peer-reviewed literature, and letters of medical justification from the provider.

⚠️ Important: Benefit exclusion appeals require strong clinical evidence. A standard appeal without supporting documentation is unlikely to succeed.
5

Consider Patient Responsibility

If the appeal is unsuccessful, inform the patient about the non-covered service and offer options such as self-pay discounts, payment plans, or alternative covered treatment options.

📞 What to Ask the Payer Representative

Essential Questions for CO-119 Denials:

  1. "What specific benefit exclusion or limitation applies to this service?"
  2. "Can you provide the exact plan document section that excludes this service?"
  3. "Are there any exceptions or waivers available for this exclusion?"
  4. "Could this service be covered under a different benefit category or code?"
  5. "Is there a medical necessity override process for non-covered benefits?"
  6. "What documentation would be required for a benefit exception appeal?"
  7. "Has this member reached their annual or lifetime benefit maximum?"
  8. "Are there alternative covered services that treat the same condition?"
  9. "What is the timeframe for filing a benefit exclusion appeal?"
  10. "May I have a reference number for this conversation?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

Reduce CO-119 Denials with These Best Practices:

  • Verify Benefits Before Service: Always check coverage for planned procedures before rendering services
  • Obtain Pre-Authorization: Secure prior authorization for services that may have coverage questions
  • Review Plan Exclusions Annually: Maintain updated knowledge of common exclusions across major payers
  • Use Benefit Verification Tools: Leverage real-time benefit verification to identify exclusions upfront
  • Document Patient ABNs: Have patients sign Advance Beneficiary Notices when services may not be covered
  • Check Formulary Lists: Verify drug and device formulary status before ordering or administering
  • Explore Alternative Codes: Determine if the service can be billed under a covered benefit category
  • Educate Providers: Train providers on common payer exclusions to avoid ordering non-covered services
  • Track Denial Patterns: Monitor which services are consistently denied as non-covered by specific payers
  • Develop Patient Policies: Create financial responsibility policies for non-covered services
