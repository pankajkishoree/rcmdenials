OA-27

OA-27 Denial Code: Payment Denied

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Coverage

📋 What is OA-27 Denial Code?

OA-27 indicates that payment denied because the patient was not eligible for coverage on the date of service.

🔍 Common Causes of OA-27 Denials

❌

Common Causes

  • Patient eligibility not verified
  • Coverage lapsed on date of service
  • Wrong insurance plan billed
  • Eligibility termination date passed

✅ How to Resolve OA-27 Denials

1

Review the Denial

Carefully review the remittance advice to understand the specific denial reason.

  • Verify patient eligibility on the date of service
  • If eligible, appeal with proof of coverage
  • If not eligible, bill correct payer or patient

📞 What to Ask the Payer

Essential Questions for OA-27 Denials:

  1. "What specific documentation is needed to resolve this denial?"
  2. "What is the appeal deadline for this denial?"
  3. "Can this claim be corrected and resubmitted?"
  4. "Is there a retro-authorization or exception process available?"

📎 Required Documentation for Appeal

  • Complete claim history with all adjustment details
  • Coordination of benefits documentation if applicable
  • All Explanation of Benefits (EOB) statements
  • Correspondence with the payer regarding this claim
  • Supporting medical records if medical necessity is questioned

💡 Prevention Strategies

  • Verify eligibility before every visit
  • Use real-time eligibility verification
  • Re-verify at check-in

⚖️ Appeal Guidance for OA-27

If you believe this denial was made in error, you have the right to appeal. Follow these steps:

  1. Review the denial thoroughly — Understand exactly why the claim was denied and what documentation is missing.
  2. Gather supporting evidence — Collect all medical records, billing documentation, and any prior authorizations.
  3. Write a detailed appeal letter — Address each denial reason specifically with supporting evidence.
  4. Submit within the deadline — Most payers require appeals within 30-180 days. Check your specific payer's timeline.
  5. Follow up regularly — Track your appeal status and be prepared to provide additional information if requested.

Essential Questions for Your Appeal:

  • "Can you explain exactly how this adjustment was calculated?"
  • "Which payer processed this claim first, and what was their payment?"
  • "Is there additional coordination needed with other insurance policies?"
  • "What specific documentation is required to resolve this adjustment?"
  • "Are there any time-sensitive actions needed to prevent further delays?"
