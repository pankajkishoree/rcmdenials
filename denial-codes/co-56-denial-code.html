CO-56

CO-56 Denial Code: Coinsurance Amount

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Patient Responsibility

📋 What is CO-56 Denial Code?

CO-56 is a Patient Responsibility denial code indicating that the payment has been adjusted for coinsurance. The patient is responsible for a percentage of the allowed amount after the deductible is met.

Coinsurance is the portion of healthcare costs that the patient pays after the deductible has been satisfied. For example, if a plan has a 20% coinsurance requirement, the patient pays 20% of the allowed amount while the payer covers the remaining 80%.

🔍 Root Causes of CO-56 Denials

🔢

Standard Coinsurance

  • Patient owes percentage per plan benefits (typically 20%)
  • Coinsurance applied after deductible satisfaction
  • Percentage calculated on allowed amount
  • Common across most insurance plans
  • Varies based on plan tier and network status
⚠️

Deductible Not Yet Met

  • Coinsurance applied before deductible satisfaction
  • Patient responsible for full amount until deductible met
  • Accumulation tracking discrepancies
  • Plan year reset confusion
  • Multiple plan coordination issues
🔄

In-Network vs Out-of-Network

  • Different coinsurance rates apply
  • In-network typically has lower coinsurance
  • Out-of-network may have higher coinsurance percentages
  • Balance billing may apply for out-of-network
  • Network tier placement affects rates
📋

Service-Specific Coinsurance

  • Certain services have separate coinsurance requirements
  • Specialty services may have unique coinsurance rules
  • Procedure-specific coinsurance percentages
  • Preventive vs diagnostic service differences
  • Medicare Part B specific coinsurance rules

✅ Step-by-Step Resolution Process

1

Verify Patient's Coinsurance Percentage

Confirm the patient's coinsurance percentage for this service type. Review the patient's benefit summary and plan documents to determine the exact coinsurance rate applicable.

💡 Pro Tip: Common coinsurance rates range from 10% to 40%, with 20% being the most typical for in-network services.
2

Confirm Deductible Status

Verify whether the deductible has been met. Check the patient's deductible accumulation to ensure coinsurance is being applied correctly. Deductible amounts reset annually for most plans.

3

Check Network Status

Verify in-network vs out-of-network coinsurance rates. Different coinsurance percentages may apply depending on the provider's network status. Ensure the correct rate was applied.

4

Validate Coinsurance Calculation

Ensure coinsurance is calculated correctly on the allowed amount. The coinsurance percentage should be applied to the payer's allowed amount, not the billed amount.

⚠️ Important: Coinsurance is calculated on the allowed amount, not the billed amount. Billed charges exceeding the allowed amount are the patient's responsibility separately.
5

File Appeal if Incorrect

If the coinsurance calculation is incorrect, file an appeal with plan documentation. Include the patient's benefit summary and any relevant plan documents to support your appeal.

6

Bill Patient for Coinsurance Balance

If the coinsurance is correctly calculated, bill the patient for the coinsurance balance. Send a clear statement showing the calculation and patient responsibility.

📞 What to Ask the Payer Representative

Essential Questions for CO-56 Denials:

  1. "What is the patient's coinsurance percentage for this service?"
  2. "Has the deductible been satisfied for this plan year?"
  3. "Is this claim processed as in-network or out-of-network?"
  4. "What is the allowed amount used for the coinsurance calculation?"
  5. "Is there a separate coinsurance rate for this specific service type?"
  6. "How does the coinsurance coordinate with other applicable adjustments?"
  7. "Is there a coinsurance maximum or out-of-pocket limit that applies?"
  8. "Can you provide the benefit summary showing the coinsurance structure?"
  9. "Are there any plan-specific rules for this coinsurance calculation?"
  10. "May I have a reference number for this conversation?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

Reduce CO-56 Denials with These Best Practices:

  • Verify Benefits Before Service: Confirm patient's coinsurance percentage and deductible status at time of service
  • Collect Copays and Coinsurance Upfront: Estimate and collect patient responsibility at the time of service
  • Use Eligibility Verification Tools: Implement real-time eligibility checks to verify benefits and deductible status
  • Understand Plan Structures: Train staff on different coinsurance structures across various payers and plan types
  • Calculate Patient Estimates: Provide patients with cost estimates before service delivery
  • Track Deductible Accumulation: Monitor patient deductible progress throughout the plan year
  • Review Explanation of Benefits: Carefully review EOBs to ensure coinsurance is calculated correctly
  • Coordinate Benefits: Verify if multiple insurance plans apply and how coinsurance coordinates
  • Educate Patients: Help patients understand their coinsurance obligations and payment options
  • Implement Payment Plans: Offer payment plans for patients with significant coinsurance balances
