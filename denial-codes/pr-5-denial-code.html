PR-5

PR-5: Deductible Amount

Published: June 26, 2026 | Last Modified: June 26, 2026

What is it?

PR-5 is a Patient Responsibility code indicating the patient is responsible for the deductible amount as per their insurance policy.

Root Causes

  • Annual deductible not met
  • High deductible plan in effect
  • Services subject to deductible

Resolution Process

  • Verify the remaining deductible amount with the payer.
  • Bill the patient for the applicable deductible portion.
  • Offer payment plans for larger deductible amounts.
  • Educate the patient on how deductibles work and their benefits.

What to Ask

Questions to ask internally or the payer to clarify the denial.

  • What is the patient's current deductible status (amount met, amount remaining)?
  • Which services are subject to the deductible under this plan?
  • Has the deductible been met for the current benefit period?

Subject: Important Information Regarding Your Recent Medical Services - Denial Code PR-5

Dear [Patient Name],

We are writing to inform you about a recent denial for services provided on [Date of Service]. The denial code associated with this claim is PR-5, which indicates that the patient is responsible for the deductible amount.

This means that your insurance plan requires you to pay a certain amount from your own funds each year for covered healthcare services before your insurance begins to pay. The amount of your unmet deductible for this service is [Specific Deductible Amount].

To resolve this, please remit payment of [Specific Deductible Amount] to our billing department. We understand that deductibles can be significant, and we offer payment plans to help manage this cost. Please contact our billing department at [Phone Number] or [Email Address] to discuss your options.

Sincerely,
[Your Clinic/Hospital Name]

Related Codes

This denial code is often related to the following codes:

PR-1, PR-2, PR-3, CO-16

Prevention

Strategies and best practices to prevent future occurrences of this denial.

  • Verify patient eligibility and deductible status at the time of service.
  • Educate patients about their high-deductible plans and the potential financial responsibility.
  • Communicate clearly with patients about services that are subject to their deductible.
  • Offer payment plan options proactively for services that will incur deductible charges.
