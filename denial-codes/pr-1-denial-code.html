PR-1

PR-1 Denial Code: Deductible Amount

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Patient Responsibility

📋 What is PR-1 Denial Code?

PR-1 indicates that the patient is responsible for paying the deductible amount. This is not a denial but an explanation that the patient has not yet met their annual deductible, and the claim amount is being applied toward that deductible.

This is one of the most common patient responsibility codes and requires proper patient billing and communication.

💰 Understanding Deductibles

What is a Deductible?

A deductible is the amount a patient must pay out-of-pocket for covered healthcare services before their insurance begins to pay. Deductibles typically reset annually on January 1st or the policy anniversary date.

Key Deductible Concepts:

  • Annual Deductible: Total amount patient must pay each year
  • Individual vs. Family: Separate deductibles for individual and family coverage
  • Embedded Deductible: Individual deductible within family plan
  • Aggregate Deductible: Family deductible must be met before anyone's coverage begins
  • Allowed Amount: Only the insurance-approved amount counts toward deductible

✅ How to Handle PR-1

1

Verify Deductible Information

Confirm the patient's deductible details from the EOB and payer.

  • Annual deductible amount
  • Amount met year-to-date
  • Amount applied from this claim
  • Remaining deductible balance
  • Deductible reset date
2

Post to Patient Account

Transfer the deductible amount from insurance to patient responsibility.

💡 Posting Tips:
  • Post insurance payment (if any)
  • Post contractual adjustment
  • Transfer deductible to patient balance
  • Add clear notes: "Applied to deductible per EOB"
3

Generate Patient Statement

Send a clear, detailed statement explaining the deductible responsibility.

  • Include copy of EOB
  • Explain deductible in plain language
  • Show deductible progress
  • List payment options
  • Provide payment deadline
4

Patient Communication

Contact patient to explain the deductible and discuss payment arrangements.

What to Say:
  • "Your insurance processed the claim correctly"
  • "The $[Amount] is applied to your $[Total] annual deductible"
  • "You've now met $[Amount] of your deductible this year"
  • "Once you meet your full deductible, insurance will cover [X]%"
  • "We offer payment plans - would that be helpful?"
5

Offer Payment Solutions

Provide flexible payment options to help patients manage deductible costs.

  • Interest-free payment plans
  • Credit card on file programs
  • Online payment portal
  • Financial assistance screening
  • Charity care programs (if eligible)

📞 What to Ask the Payer

Essential Questions for PR-1:

  1. "What is the patient's annual deductible amount?"
  2. "How much of the deductible has been met year-to-date?"
  3. "What is the remaining deductible balance?"
  4. "Is this an individual or family deductible?"
  5. "When does the deductible reset?"
  6. "Are any services exempt from the deductible?"
  7. "What is the coinsurance percentage after deductible is met?"
  8. "Is there a separate deductible for different service types?"
  9. "What is the out-of-pocket maximum?"

📎 Required Documentation for Appeal

  • Original claim with all codes and modifiers
  • Complete medical records supporting medical necessity
  • Any prior authorization or precertification documentation
  • Provider-payer contract or fee schedule reference
  • Detailed appeal letter with clinical justification

💡 Best Practices for Deductible Collection

  • Verify Benefits Upfront: Check deductible status before service
  • Collect at Time of Service: Request deductible payment at check-in when possible
  • Provide Estimates: Give patients advance notice of expected costs
  • Clear Communication: Explain deductibles in simple terms
  • Flexible Payment Plans: Offer manageable monthly payment options
  • Multiple Payment Methods: Accept various payment types
  • Online Payment Portal: Make it easy for patients to pay online
  • Prompt Billing: Send statements within 7-10 days of EOB
  • Consistent Follow-Up: Contact patients about unpaid balances regularly
  • Financial Counseling: Help patients understand their benefits
  • Track Deductible Progress: Monitor patient deductible status throughout year
  • Patient Education: Provide resources about deductibles and insurance

🎯 When Deductibles Don't Apply

Services Often Exempt from Deductible:

  • Preventive Care: Annual physicals, screenings, immunizations
  • ACA Preventive Services: Mandated preventive services at 100% coverage
  • Office Visit Copays: Some plans have copays instead of deductible
  • Generic Prescriptions: May have copay instead of deductible
  • Certain Screenings: Mammograms, colonoscopies, etc.
💡 Important: Always verify which services are subject to deductible for each patient's specific plan.

⚖️ Appeal Guidance

If you believe this denial was made in error, you have the right to appeal. Follow these steps:

  1. Review the denial thoroughly — Understand exactly why the claim was denied.
  2. Gather supporting evidence — Collect all medical records and billing documentation.
  3. Write a detailed appeal letter — Address each denial reason specifically.
  4. Submit within the deadline — Most payers require appeals within 30-180 days.
  5. Follow up regularly — Track your appeal status.

Essential Questions for Your Appeal:

  • "What specific documentation is needed to resolve this denial?"
  • "What is the appeal deadline for this denial?"
  • "Can this claim be corrected and resubmitted?"
  • "Is there a peer-to-peer review option available?"
