PR-97

PR-97 Denial Code: Payment Included in Payment for Another Claim/Service

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Patient Responsibility

📋 What is PR-97 Denial Code?

PR-97 indicates that the patient is responsible for this amount because payment for this service was included in the payment for another claim or service. This is a bundled or overlap adjustment, meaning the payer has already paid for this service under a different claim.

This code commonly arises from duplicate billing, bundled services, or coordination of benefits adjustments. Proper identification of the overlapping claim is essential to resolve this adjustment accurately.

🔍 Root Causes

Common Reasons for PR-97:

  • Duplicate Billing: Same service billed twice, resulting in overlap
  • Bundled Services: Service included in another service's payment
  • COB Adjustment: Primary payer already covered this service
  • Overlapping Claims: Multiple claims submitted for the same service period

✅ How to Handle PR-97

1

Identify Which Claim Includes the Payment

Determine which claim already includes payment for this service.

  • Review remittance advice for the overlapping claim
  • Check claim numbers and dates of service
  • Identify the payment amount already issued
  • Document the overlap for your records
2

Verify if Services Were Truly Duplicated

Confirm whether the services are genuinely overlapping or if they are distinct.

💡 Verification Checklist:
  • Compare CPT/HCPCS codes on both claims
  • Check dates of service for overlap
  • Review provider notes for each service
  • Determine if services are distinct but bundled by payer policy
3

Check if Bundling Was Appropriate

Assess whether the payer's bundling decision is correct per guidelines.

  • Review NCCI edits and bundling rules
  • Check for modifier exceptions
  • Verify medical necessity for separate billing
  • Consult payer-specific bundling policies
4

Determine if a Separate Claim is Warranted

Evaluate whether a separate claim should have been submitted or if the bundling is correct.

  • Assess clinical justification for separate services
  • Determine if modifier is needed (e.g., modifier 59)
  • Evaluate appeal potential if bundling is incorrect
  • Document rationale for any appeal
5

Adjust Billing to Remove Duplicate Charges

Correct the patient account to reflect only the appropriate balance.

  • Reverse any duplicate charges from patient account
  • Post the correct payment allocation
  • Adjust contractual adjustments as needed
  • Add clear notes explaining the adjustment
6

Notify Patient of Correct Balance

Communicate the updated balance and explanation to the patient.

Patient Communication Tips:
  • Explain the adjustment in simple terms
  • Provide the corrected balance statement
  • Offer payment options for any remaining balance
  • Document all patient communications

📞 What to Ask the Payer

Essential Questions for PR-97:

  1. "Which claim includes payment for this service?"
  2. "Can you identify the overlapping claim numbers?"
  3. "Is this a bundling or duplication issue?"
  4. "What is the exact payment amount applied from the other claim?"
  5. "Is there a modifier that would allow separate payment?"
  6. "Can you provide the remittance details for both claims?"
  7. "What documentation is needed to appeal if appropriate?"

💡 Best Practices for Handling Bundled Claims

  • Pre-Submission Review: Check for potential overlaps before billing
  • Claim Linking: Link related claims during submission
  • Modifier Usage: Apply appropriate modifiers for distinct services
  • Payer Policy Review: Understand bundling rules for each payer
  • Documentation: Maintain detailed records of all services
  • Regular Auditing: Audit claims for duplicate submissions
  • Staff Training: Train billing staff on bundling rules
  • System Checks: Implement automated duplicate detection
  • Clear Communication: Document all payer interactions
  • Appeal Process: Know when and how to appeal bundling decisions
