CO-90

CO-90 Denial Code: Laterality Mismatch

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Contractual Obligation

📋 What is CO-90 Denial Code?

CO-90 is a Contractual Obligation (CO) denial code indicating a laterality mismatch. This means the claim specifies a side of the body (left, right, or bilateral) that is inconsistent with the procedure code, diagnosis, or payer policy.

Accurate laterality coding is essential for procedures performed on paired sites or organs, ensuring correct medical necessity and payment.

🔍 Root Causes of CO-90 Denials

↔️

Incorrect Laterality Code

  • Using 'LT' (Left) when service was on the Right side.
  • Using 'RT' (Right) when service was on the Left side.
  • Using '50' (Bilateral) modifier when service was unilateral.
  • Unilateral procedure coded as bilateral without modifier 50.
❓

Diagnosis Mismatch

  • Diagnosis only affects one side of the body, but procedure is coded bilaterally.
  • Laterality of diagnosis not clearly documented.
📝

Documentation Gaps

  • Physician notes do not specify left, right, or bilateral.
  • Ambiguous documentation leading to coding assumptions.
⚙️

Payer Policy Conflicts

  • Payer policy requires specific laterality modifiers not used.
  • Payer edits flagging inconsistent laterality.

✅ Step-by-Step Resolution Process

1

Review the Remittance Advice (RA)

Examine the ERA/EOB for CO-90. Identify the specific diagnosis and procedure codes flagged and any remark codes providing detail about the laterality issue.

2

Verify Physician Documentation

Consult the patient's medical records, especially the physician's notes for the date of service. Confirm whether the procedure was performed on the left, right, or bilaterally.

  • Look for explicit mentions of 'left', 'right', 'bilateral', 'LT', 'RT', '50'.
  • Check for diagrams or notes indicating site of service.
3

Check Coding Guidelines and Payer Policies

Refer to CPT guidelines for laterality modifiers (LT, RT, 50). Review payer policies for specific requirements on coding procedures performed on paired sites.

💡 Pro Tip: Modifier 50 (Bilateral Procedure) is used when a procedure is performed bilaterally at the same session. Different modifiers may apply for staged bilateral procedures.
4

Correct the Claim

Update the claim with the correct laterality code or modifier based on physician documentation and payer guidelines. If a unilateral procedure was performed, ensure only one side is billed unless modifier 50 is appropriate.

⚠️ Important: Ensure consistent laterality is used across all submitted codes (diagnosis, procedure, modifiers).
5

Resubmit and Follow Up

Resubmit the corrected claim adheresing to the payer's timely filing limits. Confirm the claim is processed correctly by following up.

📞 What to Ask the Payer Representative

Essential Questions for CO-90 Denials:

  1. "Can you confirm the reason for the CO-90 denial on this claim?"
  2. "Which code(s) on the claim have a laterality mismatch?"
  3. "What is the correct laterality code or modifier expected for this procedure?"
  4. "Does the diagnosis support a bilateral procedure in this case?"
  5. "Are there specific documentation requirements for laterality coding for this procedure?"
  6. "Can you refer me to the payer's policy on laterality for the services billed?"
  7. "Should this procedure have been submitted on two separate claims for left and right sides?"
  8. "Is diagnosis code [Specific Diagnosis Code] compatible with a bilateral procedure?"
  9. "What is the timely filing limit for resubmitting this corrected claim?"
  10. "Can you provide a reference number for this call?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

Prevent CO-90 Denials with These Practices:

  • Precise Documentation: Ensure physician notes clearly specify the side (left, right) or if the procedure was bilateral.
  • Accurate Modifier Use: Properly apply modifiers LT, RT, or 50 based on documentation and guidelines.
  • Understand Payer Policies: Review payer guidelines for laterality requirements for specific procedures.
  • Coding Audits: Regularly audit claims for correct laterality coding.
  • Provider Training: Educate providers on the importance of specific laterality documentation.
  • Cross-Reference Codes: Ensure diagnosis codes are consistent with the laterality of the procedure.
