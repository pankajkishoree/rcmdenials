PR-17

PR-17: Amount Applied to Another Provider's Service

Published: June 26, 2026 | Last Modified: June 26, 2026

What is it?

PR-17 is a Patient Responsibility code indicating that the payer applied the amount to another provider's service. This often suggests a Coordination of Benefits (COB) issue or an error in claim submission.

Root Causes

  • Patient billed for services rendered by another provider.
  • Coordination of Benefits (COB) errors.
  • Incorrect patient identifier or insurance information used.
  • Payer misinterpreting claim or patient's insurance coverage.

Resolution Process

  • Verify the patient's current insurance details and coverage.
  • Investigate potential COB issues by contacting the patient and/or payers.
  • Correct the claim with the accurate patient identifier, insurance information, and provider sequence if applicable.
  • Resubmit the corrected claim.

What to Ask

Questions to ask internally or the payer to clarify the denial.

  • Which other provider's service is the payer referencing?
  • What is the patient's other insurance information, and what is its coordination order?
  • Is there an error in the patient's demographic or insurance information on the claim?

[Date]

[Patient Name]
[Patient Address]

Subject: Important Information Regarding Your Recent Medical Services - Denial Code PR-17

Dear [Patient Name],

We are writing to address a denial for services provided on [Date of Service]. The denial code is PR-17, indicating that the payer has applied the amount to another provider's service. This may point to a Coordination of Benefits (COB) issue or incorrect billing information.

To resolve this, we need to ensure your insurance information is accurate and determine the correct order of benefits if you have multiple insurance plans. Please provide us with details of any other insurance coverage you may have, including policy numbers and dates of service where these were active.

Once we have this information, we will correct and resubmit the claim. Please contact our billing department at [Phone Number] or [Email Address] with any relevant details or questions.

Sincerely,
[Your Clinic/Hospital Name]

Related Codes

Prevention

Strategies and best practices to prevent future occurrences of this denial.

  • Accurately capture and verify all patient insurance policies, including primary and secondary coverage, at registration.
  • Thoroughly investigate and document Coordination of Benefits (COB) information.
  • Ensure correct patient identifiers and insurance information are submitted on claims.
  • Train staff on identifying potential COB conflicts and resolution steps.
