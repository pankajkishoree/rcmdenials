CO-22

CO-22 Denial Code: This Care May Be Covered by Another Payer

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Coordination of Benefits

📋 What is CO-22 Denial Code?

CO-22 indicates that the payer believes another insurance carrier should be primary for this claim, or that coordination of benefits (COB) information is needed. This denial occurs when there's a question about which payer should pay first.

This is common when patients have multiple insurance policies (dual coverage, Medicare + supplemental, etc.) and the payer order needs clarification.

🔍 Understanding Coordination of Benefits (COB)

1️⃣

Primary vs. Secondary

  • Primary payer pays first
  • Secondary payer pays after primary
  • Tertiary payer (if applicable) pays last
  • COB rules determine payer order
  • Patient cannot choose payer order
👨‍👩‍👧

Common COB Scenarios

  • Patient has employer + spouse's insurance
  • Medicare + Medicare Supplement
  • Medicare + Medicaid (dual eligible)
  • Parent's insurance for dependent child
  • Workers' comp + health insurance
  • Auto insurance + health insurance
⚖️

COB Determination Rules

  • Birthday Rule: Parent with earlier birthday is primary for child
  • Active/Retired: Active coverage is primary over retiree
  • COBRA: COBRA is always secondary
  • Medicare: Specific rules based on employment status
  • Liability: Auto/WC is primary over health insurance
📝

Why CO-22 Occurs

  • Payer has record of other insurance
  • Patient didn't disclose dual coverage
  • COB information not updated
  • Claim filed to wrong payer first
  • Missing primary payer EOB

✅ How to Resolve CO-22 Denials

1

Verify Patient's Insurance Coverage

Contact the patient to confirm all active insurance policies and determine correct payer order.

Questions to Ask Patient:
  • "Do you have any other health insurance?"
  • "Are you covered under a spouse's or parent's plan?"
  • "Do you have Medicare and a supplement plan?"
  • "Was this injury work-related or from an auto accident?"
  • "Are you currently employed? Is your spouse employed?"
2

Determine Correct Payer Order

Apply COB rules to identify which payer should be primary.

COB Quick Reference:
  • Dual Employer Coverage: Patient's own employer plan is primary
  • Dependent Child: Birthday rule applies (earlier birthday = primary)
  • Medicare + Employer (65+): If employer has 20+ employees, employer is primary
  • Medicare + ESRD: Employer is primary for first 30 months
  • Auto/WC Accident: Liability insurance is always primary
3

File Claim with Correct Primary Payer

If claim was filed to wrong payer, submit to the correct primary payer first.

  • Verify primary payer eligibility
  • Submit claim to primary payer
  • Wait for primary payer EOB
  • Track claim through adjudication
4

Submit to Secondary Payer with Primary EOB

Once primary payer processes the claim, submit to secondary with primary EOB attached.

💡 Pro Tip: Include the primary payer's EOB and payment information when filing to secondary. Use claim adjustment reason codes from primary EOB.
5

Update COB Information

Notify all payers of the correct insurance information to prevent future denials.

  • Submit COB questionnaire to payers
  • Update patient demographics in billing system
  • Document all insurance policies
  • Verify COB at each visit

📞 What to Ask the Payer

Essential Questions for CO-22 Denials:

  1. "What other insurance do you have on file for this patient?"
  2. "Why do you believe you are not the primary payer?"
  3. "What information do you need to process this claim?"
  4. "Can you provide the name and policy number of the other insurance?"
  5. "If we provide proof you are primary, will you reprocess the claim?"
  6. "Do you need a COB questionnaire completed?"
  7. "What is your process for updating COB information?"
  8. "If another payer is primary, should I submit the claim there first?"

🎯 Special COB Scenarios

Medicare + Employer (Working Aged)

Rule: If employer has 20+ employees, employer plan is primary. Medicare is secondary.

Action: File with employer first, then Medicare with EOB.

Medicare + Medicaid (Dual Eligible)

Rule: Medicare is always primary. Medicaid is secondary.

Action: File Medicare first. Medicaid automatically receives crossover.

Divorced Parents

Rule: Custodial parent's plan is primary. Non-custodial parent's plan is secondary.

Action: Verify custody arrangement and file accordingly.

Auto Accident

Rule: Auto insurance (PIP/no-fault) is primary. Health insurance is secondary.

Action: File with auto insurance first, even if health insurance is patient's primary.

Workers' Compensation

Rule: Workers' comp is primary for work-related injuries. Health insurance doesn't pay.

Action: File with workers' comp only. Don't bill health insurance.

COBRA Coverage

Rule: COBRA is always secondary to any other group coverage.

Action: File with active group plan first, then COBRA.

💡 Prevention Strategies

  • Verify at Every Visit: Ask about insurance changes at each appointment
  • Complete COB Forms: Have patients fill out detailed insurance questionnaires
  • Check Eligibility: Run eligibility checks on all known insurance policies
  • Document Everything: Keep detailed records of insurance verification
  • Update Payer Records: Notify payers immediately of COB changes
  • Train Front Desk: Educate staff on COB rules and proper verification
  • Use COB Software: Implement tools that automatically determine payer order
  • Patient Education: Explain importance of disclosing all insurance
  • Annual Review: Verify insurance information annually for all patients
