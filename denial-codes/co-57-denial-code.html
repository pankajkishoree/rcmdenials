CO-57

CO-57 Denial Code: Fee Schedule Dollar Amount Exceeded

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Fee Schedule

📋 What is CO-57 Denial Code?

CO-57 is a Fee Schedule denial code indicating that the charge exceeds the payer's fee schedule or the maximum allowable amount for the service. The payer will only pay up to the fee schedule amount.

This denial occurs when the billed amount is higher than what the payer allows for that specific procedure code. The provider must either accept the fee schedule amount as payment in full or appeal with supporting documentation.

🔍 Root Causes of CO-57 Denials

🔢

Charges Above Fee Schedule

  • Billed amount exceeds contracted/allowed rate
  • Provider's charges exceed payer's maximum allowable
  • Fee schedule rates updated but provider unaware
  • Contractual rate adjustments not reflected in billing
  • Charge master rates exceed payer allowances
⚠️

Non-Contracted Rates

  • Provider not on payer's fee schedule
  • No contracted rate established with payer
  • Out-of-network provider billing rates
  • New provider not yet credentialed with payer
  • Contract termination or lapse in network status
🔄

RBRVS Adjustments

  • Medicare RBRVS payment less than billed
  • Resource-Based Relative Value Scale applied
  • Geographic practice cost index adjustments
  • Conversion factor applied to relative values
  • Annual RBRVS updates affecting payment
📋

Multiple Pricing Methodologies

  • Different fee schedules applied
  • Medicare MPFS vs commercial fee schedules
  • APG, APC, or DRG payment methodologies
  • Bundled payment arrangements
  • Case rate or per diem payments

✅ Step-by-Step Resolution Process

1

Verify Contracted Fee Schedule Rate

Verify the contracted fee schedule rate for this CPT code. Review the provider's contract with the payer to determine the agreed-upon fee schedule and allowed amounts.

💡 Pro Tip: Fee schedules can be based on Medicare MPFS, custom contracted rates, or proprietary payer fee schedules. Identify which applies to your contract.
2

Compare Billed vs Allowed Amount

Compare the billed amount versus the allowed amount. Calculate the difference to understand the financial impact and determine if the fee schedule rate is acceptable or if an appeal is warranted.

3

Confirm Correct CPT Code and Modifiers

Confirm that the correct CPT code and modifiers were billed. Ensure the procedure code is accurate and that any applicable modifiers have been properly applied to reflect the service provided.

4

Check for Different Fee Schedule

Check if a different fee schedule should apply. Determine if there are multiple fee schedules available and whether a more favorable schedule could be applied to this claim.

⚠️ Important: Some payers apply different fee schedules based on service location, provider type, or patient plan. Verify the correct schedule for this specific scenario.
5

Adjust Claim or Accept Adjustment

Adjust the claim to reflect the correct fee schedule amount or accept the contractual adjustment. If the fee schedule is accurate, write off the difference as a contractual adjustment.

6

Appeal with Contract Documentation

If the fee schedule is incorrect or the wrong schedule was applied, appeal with contract documentation. Provide the provider's contract with the payer to support the appeal.

📞 What to Ask the Payer Representative

Essential Questions for CO-57 Denials:

  1. "What fee schedule was used to process this claim?"
  2. "What is the allowed amount for this procedure code?"
  3. "Is there a contracted rate for this provider with your organization?"
  4. "Can you provide the fee schedule reference or documentation?"
  5. "Is this based on Medicare MPFS or a proprietary fee schedule?"
  6. "Are there any modifiers that could affect the fee schedule application?"
  7. "What is the effective date of the fee schedule being applied?"
  8. "Is there a different fee schedule that should apply based on the service location?"
  9. "What is the appeals process for fee schedule disputes?"
  10. "May I have a reference number for this conversation?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

Reduce CO-57 Denials with These Best Practices:

  • Review Fee Schedules Regularly: Stay updated on payer fee schedule changes and contract renegotiations
  • Use Charge Capture Tools: Implement software that automatically checks charges against fee schedules
  • Maintain Contract Documentation: Keep organized records of all payer contracts and fee schedules
  • Verify Rates Before Billing: Confirm the correct fee schedule before claim submission
  • Track Fee Schedule Updates: Monitor annual and quarterly fee schedule updates from payers
  • Understand Payment Methodologies: Train staff on different fee schedule types (MPFS, RBRVS, contracted rates)
  • Implement Pre-Bill Edits: Use claim scrubbers that validate charges against fee schedules
  • Review Denial Patterns: Analyze CO-57 denials to identify fee schedule discrepancies
  • Negotiate Competitive Rates: Ensure contracted rates are competitive and reflect market standards
  • Document All Agreements: Maintain written documentation of all fee schedule agreements and amendments
