CO-253

CO-253 Denial Code: Sequestration — Loss in Federal Spending Payment

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Federal Mandate

📋 What is CO-253 Denial Code?

CO-253 is a Contractual Obligation (CO) denial code indicating that payment has been reduced due to sequestration — automatic federal spending reductions that apply to Medicare and Medicaid payments. This is not a claim error.

The sequestration reduction stems from the Budget Control Act of 2011, which mandated automatic across-the-board spending cuts. For Medicare, this typically results in a 2% reduction in payment. This adjustment is applied at the payment level and cannot be appealed.

🔍 Root Causes of CO-253 Denials

🏛️

Federal Sequestration

  • Budget Control Act of 2011 automatic cuts
  • Mandatory federal spending reductions
  • Applied across all federal programs
  • Annual congressional action required to suspend
  • Non-discretionary spending adjustment
💊

Medicare Payment Reduction

  • 2% across-the-board reduction
  • Applied to all Medicare Part A and Part B claims
  • Includes Medicare Advantage payments
  • Reduction calculated at payment processing
  • Appears as separate line item on remittance
📊

Mandatory Spending Cuts

  • Applied to all Medicare claims automatically
  • Cannot be waived or exempted
  • Consistent across all providers
  • Government-mandated adjustment
  • No provider or patient discretion
🚫

Non-Appealable

  • Cannot be appealed as it is federal law
  • No administrative review process
  • Congressional action required for changes
  • Not subject to payer discretion
  • Permanent until law is changed

✅ Step-by-Step Resolution Process

1

Recognize This Is a Federal Mandate, Not a Claim Error

Understand that CO-253 is not a denial based on claim errors, coding issues, or missing documentation. It is a federally mandated payment reduction that applies to all Medicare claims. No corrective action on the claim itself will change this adjustment.

💡 Pro Tip: Do not waste time appealing CO-253 adjustments. This is a statutory requirement and cannot be overturned through the appeals process.
2

Verify the Sequestration Reduction Percentage Was Applied Correctly

Review the remittance advice to confirm the correct sequestration percentage was applied. The standard reduction is 2%, but verify this matches the current federal mandate. Compare the expected payment with the actual payment after the adjustment.

3

Ensure Your Billing System Accounts for the Reduction

Update your billing and financial systems to automatically account for the sequestration reduction on Medicare claims. This ensures accurate revenue forecasting and prevents discrepancies between expected and actual payments.

4

Do Not Appeal — It Cannot Be Overturned

Do not submit an appeal for CO-253 adjustments. This is a federal law-mandated reduction and the appeals process does not apply. Submitting an appeal will only waste time and resources.

⚠️ Important: Appeals for sequestration adjustments will be automatically denied. Focus your appeals resources on other denial types that can be overturned.
5

Adjust Expected Payment Amounts Accordingly

Factor the 2% sequestration reduction into your revenue cycle management. Adjust expected payment amounts for Medicare claims to reflect the reduced reimbursement. This helps maintain accurate financial projections.

6

Document for Financial Reporting

Maintain clear documentation of sequestration adjustments for financial reporting purposes. Track the total impact of sequestration on your revenue and report it as a federal mandate reduction, not a denial or write-off.

📞 What to Ask the Payer Representative

Essential Questions for CO-253 Denials:

  1. "Can you confirm the sequestration percentage applied to this claim?"
  2. "Is this reduction correct per current federal mandate?"
  3. "When was this reduction first applied to Medicare claims?"
  4. "Is there any way to get an exemption from sequestration?"
  5. "Where can I find official documentation about this reduction?"
  6. "Does this apply to all Medicare claim types equally?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

Manage CO-253 Sequestration Adjustments with These Best Practices:

  • Update Revenue Projections: Factor the 2% sequestration reduction into all Medicare revenue forecasts
  • Automate Adjustments: Configure billing systems to automatically account for sequestration on Medicare claims
  • Train Staff on Federal Mandates: Ensure billing staff understand that CO-253 is not an appealable denial
  • Track Cumulative Impact: Monitor the total financial impact of sequestration across all Medicare claims
  • Document for Audits: Maintain clear records showing sequestration adjustments are federally mandated
  • Monitor Legislative Changes: Stay informed about any congressional action that may suspend or modify sequestration
  • Separate from Denials Reporting: Report sequestration separately from other denial categories in financial reporting
  • Educate Providers: Inform healthcare providers that sequestration is a federal requirement, not a payer error
  • Plan for Budget Impact: Incorporate sequestration into annual budget planning and financial modeling
  • Maintain Compliance Records: Keep documentation of sequestration adjustments for regulatory compliance purposes
