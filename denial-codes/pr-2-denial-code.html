PR-2

PR-2 Denial Code: Coinsurance Amount

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Patient Responsibility

📋 What is PR-2 Denial Code?

PR-2 is a Patient Responsibility (PR) code indicating that the patient is responsible for the coinsurance amount. Coinsurance is a percentage of the allowed amount that the patient must pay after the deductible has been satisfied. This is not a traditional denial but rather a patient financial responsibility that must be billed to the patient.

The patient's portion is calculated based on the plan's coinsurance percentage (typically 10-40%) applied to the allowed amount after the deductible has been met. This amount should be collected from the patient according to the provider's financial policy.

🔍 Root Causes of PR-2 Balances

📊

Plan Coinsurance Percentage

  • Standard 80/20 plan with 20% patient coinsurance
  • 70/30 plan with 30% patient coinsurance
  • High-deductible plan with 100% coinsurance up to OOP max
  • Copay-only plans may not apply
  • Plan-specific coinsurance tiers
🏢

Out-of-Network Coinsurance

  • Higher coinsurance for out-of-network providers
  • Balance billing allowed beyond coinsurance
  • OON deductible and coinsurance separate from in-network
  • Higher patient financial responsibility
  • Non-contracted rate applied to allowed amount
📋

Tiered Benefits

  • Provider tier affects coinsurance level
  • Preferred vs. non-preferred tier coinsurance
  • Reference-based pricing adjustment
  • Facility vs. professional service coinsurance
  • Site of service coinsurance differences
💳

Deductible Met Status

  • Coinsurance begins only after deductible met
  • Full allowed amount may apply before deductible met
  • Partial year deductible reset affects coinsurance
  • Family vs. individual deductible impact
  • Embedded vs. non-embedded deductible rules

✅ Step-by-Step Resolution Process

1

Review the EOB/ERA Details

Examine the remittance advice to verify the coinsurance percentage applied, the allowed amount used, and whether the deductible was met before coinsurance was calculated.

💡 Pro Tip: Always verify that the coinsurance percentage matches the patient's plan benefits. Payer systems can sometimes apply incorrect percentages.
2

Verify Patient Benefit Plan

Confirm the patient's benefit plan details including the coinsurance percentage, deductible status, out-of-pocket maximum, and whether the provider is in-network or out-of-network.

3

Check Contractual Write-Offs

If in-network, verify that the contractual adjustment was applied correctly and the coinsurance was calculated on the correct allowed amount. Discrepancies should be raised with the payer.

⚠️ Important: For in-network providers, you cannot bill the patient more than the contracted coinsurance amount. Balance billing is prohibited.
4

Bill the Patient

Send a patient statement clearly showing the coinsurance amount due. Include the EOB/ERA as documentation and reference the PR-2 adjustment code. Offer payment plan options if needed.

5

Track and Follow Up

Monitor patient payment on the coinsurance balance. Follow up with the patient at 30, 60, and 90 days if unpaid. Consider collection procedures per your financial policy.

📞 What to Ask the Payer Representative

Essential Questions for PR-2 Balances:

  1. "What coinsurance percentage was applied to this claim?"
  2. "Has the patient met their deductible for this benefit period?"
  3. "What is the patient's current out-of-pocket maximum status?"
  4. "Is this in-network or out-of-network coinsurance rate?"
  5. "What was the allowed amount used for the coinsurance calculation?"
  6. "Are there any discounts or adjustments that should be applied?"
  7. "Does the patient have a secondary insurance that could cover this coinsurance?"
  8. "Is this a tiered benefit where a different provider tier should apply?"
  9. "Can you provide a detailed benefit summary for this patient?"
  10. "May I have a reference number for this conversation?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

Reduce PR-2 Collection Challenges with These Best Practices:

  • Estimate Coinsurance Upfront: Calculate estimated patient coinsurance at time of service scheduling
  • Collect at Time of Service: Request estimated coinsurance payment at check-in or check-out
  • Communicate Financial Policy: Have patients sign a financial policy agreeing to pay coinsurance amounts
  • Verify Benefits Before Service: Check deductible status and coinsurance percentage before each visit
  • Offer Payment Plans: Establish structured payment options for large coinsurance balances
  • Check for Secondary Insurance: Ask patients if they have secondary coverage that may cover coinsurance
  • Use Patient Portals: Enable online payment and balance viewing to improve collection rates
  • Send Timely Statements: Bill patients promptly after receiving the ERA to avoid confusion
  • Provide Clear Explanations: Include EOB copies and simple explanations of coinsurance on statements
  • Track Patient AR Aging: Monitor patient accounts receivable and follow up on overdue balances
