CO-16

CO-16 Denial Code: Claim Lacks Information or Has Submission/Billing Error

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Contractual Obligation

📋 What is CO-16 Denial Code?

CO-16 is a Contractual Obligation (CO) denial code indicating that the claim lacks necessary information or contains a submission/billing error. This is one of the most common denial codes in medical billing, accounting for approximately 15-20% of all claim denials.

The payer has identified missing, incomplete, or incorrect information that prevents proper claim adjudication. This denial requires corrective action and claim resubmission.

🔍 Root Causes of CO-16 Denials

📝

Missing Information

  • Missing diagnosis codes (ICD-10)
  • Incomplete patient demographics
  • Missing referring physician NPI
  • Absent authorization numbers
  • Missing place of service codes
❌

Incorrect Data Entry

  • Wrong patient date of birth
  • Incorrect member ID number
  • Invalid provider NPI
  • Mismatched name spelling
  • Wrong insurance group number
🔢

Coding Errors

  • Invalid CPT/HCPCS codes
  • Incorrect modifier usage
  • Missing required modifiers
  • Diagnosis-procedure mismatch
  • Outdated code sets
📄

Documentation Issues

  • Missing medical records
  • Incomplete operative reports
  • Absent physician signatures
  • Missing itemized bills
  • Incomplete claim forms

✅ Step-by-Step Resolution Process

1

Review the Remittance Advice

Carefully examine the ERA/EOB to identify the specific missing or incorrect information. Look for remark codes that provide additional details about the denial reason.

💡 Pro Tip: Common remark codes with CO-16 include MA130 (missing diagnosis), N115 (missing authorization), and M20 (missing/invalid HCPCS).
2

Verify Patient Eligibility

Confirm the patient's insurance coverage was active on the date of service. Verify demographic information matches the payer's records exactly.

  • Run real-time eligibility check
  • Verify member ID and group number
  • Confirm patient name spelling
  • Check date of birth accuracy
3

Gather Missing Information

Collect all required data elements identified in the denial. This may require contacting the provider, reviewing medical records, or obtaining additional documentation.

4

Correct the Claim

Update the claim with accurate information. Use frequency code 7 (replacement claim) and include the original claim number (DCN/ICN) in the appropriate field.

⚠️ Important: Do not submit as a new claim. Always use frequency code 7 to indicate this is a corrected resubmission.
5

Resubmit Within Timely Filing

Submit the corrected claim within the payer's timely filing limit. Most payers allow 90-365 days from the original denial date for corrected claims.

6

Document and Follow Up

Record all actions taken in your billing system. Set a follow-up date 14-21 days after resubmission to verify claim processing.

📞 What to Ask the Payer Representative

Essential Questions for CO-16 Denials:

  1. "What specific information is missing or incorrect on this claim?"
  2. "Can you provide the exact remark codes associated with this denial?"
  3. "What is the correct format for the missing information?"
  4. "Is there a specific field or box number on the claim form that needs correction?"
  5. "What is the timely filing deadline for resubmitting this corrected claim?"
  6. "Should I submit this as a corrected claim (frequency code 7) or an appeal?"
  7. "Do you need any supporting documentation with the corrected claim?"
  8. "Can you confirm the patient's eligibility was active on the date of service?"
  9. "Is there a specific department or address for corrected claim submissions?"
  10. "May I have a reference number for this call?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

Reduce CO-16 Denials with These Best Practices:

  • Implement Front-End Verification: Verify insurance eligibility and benefits before service delivery
  • Use Claim Scrubbing Software: Automated tools catch missing information before submission
  • Standardize Data Entry: Create templates and checklists for consistent claim preparation
  • Train Staff Regularly: Keep billing team updated on payer-specific requirements
  • Maintain Clean Provider Data: Ensure all NPI numbers and provider information are current
  • Verify Diagnosis Codes: Confirm ICD-10 codes are valid and specific to the highest level
  • Double-Check Modifiers: Ensure appropriate modifiers are used and properly sequenced
  • Review Before Submission: Implement a quality check process before electronic submission
  • Track Denial Patterns: Identify recurring issues and address root causes
  • Update Payer Requirements: Stay informed about payer policy changes and new requirements
