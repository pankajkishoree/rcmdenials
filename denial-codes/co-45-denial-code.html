CO-45

CO-45 Denial Code: Charges Exceed Contracted/Legislated Fee Arrangement

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Contractual Obligation

📋 What is CO-45 Denial Code?

CO-45 indicates that the charges submitted exceed the contracted or legislated fee arrangement between the provider and payer. This is a contractual adjustment, not a true denial, meaning the payer is paying according to the agreed-upon fee schedule.

The difference between your billed charges and the allowed amount is written off as a contractual adjustment. This code is informational and typically does not require action unless you believe the contracted rate was applied incorrectly.

🔍 Root Causes of CO-45 Adjustments

💰

Billing Above Contract

  • Charges exceed contracted rates
  • Fee schedule not updated in billing system
  • Billed at standard rates vs. contracted rates
  • Incorrect charge master pricing
📄

Contract Issues

  • Payer applied wrong fee schedule
  • Outdated contract rates in payer system
  • Incorrect provider network tier
  • Contract renegotiation not updated
🔢

Coding Discrepancies

  • Wrong procedure code billed
  • Missing or incorrect modifiers
  • Bundled services billed separately
  • Upcoding detected by payer
⚖️

Regulatory Rates

  • Medicare fee schedule limits
  • Medicaid state-mandated rates
  • Workers' comp fee schedules
  • No-fault insurance limits

✅ When to Take Action on CO-45

1

Verify the Contracted Rate

Pull your contract with the payer and verify the allowed amount matches your contracted rate for the specific CPT code and modifier combination.

💡 Action Required If: The allowed amount is less than your contracted rate, or the wrong fee schedule was applied.
2

Check for Coding Errors

Ensure the correct CPT/HCPCS code was billed with appropriate modifiers. Incorrect coding can trigger lower reimbursement rates.

  • Verify procedure code accuracy
  • Confirm modifier usage
  • Check for bundling issues
  • Review place of service code
3

Contact Payer for Clarification

If you believe the rate is incorrect, call the payer's provider relations or claims department to discuss the discrepancy.

Questions to Ask:
  1. "What fee schedule was applied to this claim?"
  2. "Can you confirm our contracted rate for CPT code [XXXXX]?"
  3. "Was the correct provider network tier applied?"
  4. "Are there any modifiers that would affect the reimbursement rate?"
  5. "Can you provide a copy of the fee schedule you're using?"
4

File an Appeal if Necessary

If the payer confirms an error, submit a formal appeal with supporting documentation including your contract and fee schedule.

Per our contract effective [Date] (Contract #[Number]), the agreed-upon rate for CPT [Code] is $[Amount]. The claim was processed at $[Amount], resulting in an underpayment of $[Difference].

Enclosed please find:
1. Copy of relevant contract pages showing fee schedule
2. Original remittance advice
3. Claim detail

Sincerely,
[Name]

💡 Prevention Strategies

  • Maintain Updated Fee Schedules: Keep contracted rates current in your billing system
  • Regular Contract Reviews: Audit payer contracts annually and after renegotiations
  • Monitor Reimbursement Rates: Track allowed amounts to identify discrepancies early
  • Verify Network Status: Confirm provider network tier and participation status
  • Code Accurately: Ensure correct CPT codes and modifiers to avoid rate reductions
