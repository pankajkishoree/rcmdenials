CO-29

CO-29 Denial Code: Time Limit for Filing Has Expired

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Contractual Obligation | Priority: Critical

📋 What is CO-29 Denial Code?

CO-29 is one of the most challenging denial codes, indicating that the claim was submitted after the payer's timely filing deadline. This means the claim was filed too late according to the contracted or regulatory time limit.

⚠️ Critical: CO-29 denials are often difficult to overturn and may result in write-offs. However, there are specific circumstances where appeals can be successful.

🔍 Root Causes of CO-29 Denials

📅

Delayed Submission

  • Claims held in pending status too long
  • Waiting for medical records
  • Authorization delays
  • Backlog in billing department
  • Staff turnover or training issues
🔄

Resubmission Issues

  • Original claim denied, corrected claim filed late
  • Multiple denial/resubmission cycles
  • Payer lost original claim
  • Electronic submission failures
  • Clearinghouse delays
📝

Documentation Delays

  • Late charge entry by providers
  • Delayed operative reports
  • Missing physician signatures
  • Incomplete medical records
  • Coordination of benefits delays
❌

Process Failures

  • No tracking system for filing deadlines
  • Incorrect payer filing limits in system
  • Claims not prioritized by deadline
  • No alerts for approaching deadlines
  • Poor workflow management

✅ How to Appeal CO-29 Denials

1

Verify the Filing Deadline

Confirm the payer's timely filing limit and calculate whether the claim was truly filed late.

  • Check your contract for filing deadlines
  • Verify date of service
  • Confirm original submission date
  • Check for any extensions or exceptions
💡 Pro Tip: Request proof of original submission date from your clearinghouse or payer portal.
2

Identify Valid Appeal Reasons

CO-29 appeals are only successful under specific circumstances:

Valid Reasons for Appeal:
  • Payer Error: Payer lost or delayed processing original timely claim
  • Corrected Claim: Original claim was timely; this is a corrected resubmission
  • System Failure: Clearinghouse or electronic submission failure
  • Retroactive Eligibility: Patient eligibility determined after DOS
  • Coordination of Benefits: Waiting for primary payer payment
  • Payer Delay: Payer requested additional information causing delay
  • Natural Disaster: Filing delayed due to declared emergency
3

Gather Supporting Documentation

Collect evidence to support your appeal:

  • Proof of original timely submission (clearinghouse report, confirmation number)
  • Correspondence from payer requesting additional information
  • Primary payer EOB (for COB situations)
  • Eligibility verification showing retroactive coverage
  • System error reports or clearinghouse rejection notices
4

File Appeal Immediately

Submit your appeal as soon as possible - most payers have strict appeal deadlines (30-60 days).

⚠️ Critical: Missing the appeal deadline will result in automatic denial with no further recourse.
5

Escalate if Necessary

If initial appeal is denied, consider escalation options:

  • Request supervisor review
  • File second-level appeal
  • Contact provider relations representative
  • File complaint with state insurance commissioner
  • Consider external review (if available)

📞 What to Ask the Payer

Essential Questions for CO-29 Denials:

  1. "What is your timely filing limit for this type of claim?"
  2. "What date are you using as the date of service?"
  3. "What date did you receive the original claim submission?"
  4. "Do you have record of our original timely submission on [date]?"
  5. "Was this claim previously denied and resubmitted as a corrected claim?"
  6. "Are there any exceptions to your timely filing policy?"
  7. "What is the deadline for filing an appeal of this denial?"
  8. "What documentation do you need to support a timely filing appeal?"
  9. "Is there a different filing limit for corrected claims?"
  10. "Can you provide written confirmation of your timely filing policy?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

Eliminate Timely Filing Denials:

  • Implement Deadline Tracking: Use billing software with automated timely filing alerts
  • Submit Claims Promptly: Target submission within 7-14 days of service
  • Prioritize by Deadline: Work claims with shortest filing limits first
  • Monitor Aging Reports: Review claims aging daily, focusing on approaching deadlines
  • Maintain Payer Database: Keep updated list of all payer filing deadlines
  • Electronic Submission: Use electronic claims for faster submission and proof of receipt
  • Save Confirmation Reports: Archive all submission confirmations for at least 7 years
  • Set Internal Deadlines: Create internal deadlines 30 days before payer deadline
  • Escalation Process: Establish protocol for claims approaching deadline
  • Staff Training: Educate team on importance of timely filing
  • Charge Entry Deadlines: Require providers to submit charges within 48 hours
  • Regular Audits: Review timely filing denial rates monthly
