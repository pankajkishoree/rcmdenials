CO-167

CO-167 Denial Code: This (These) Diagnosis(es) Is(Are) Not Covered

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Diagnosis

📋 What is CO-167 Denial Code?

CO-167 is a denial code indicating that the diagnosis code(s) submitted are not covered under the patient's benefit plan for the services rendered. Payment is adjusted because the payer's coverage guidelines exclude the specific diagnoses submitted on the claim.

This denial means the patient's insurance plan does not provide coverage for treatment related to the submitted diagnosis codes, regardless of whether the service itself is a covered benefit.

🔍 Root Causes of CO-167 Denials

🚫

Non-Covered Diagnosis

  • ICD-10 code not covered by payer
  • Diagnosis falls outside plan benefits
  • Payer excludes specific condition codes
  • Experimental/investigational diagnosis
  • Diagnosis not recognized by payer
📋

Benefit Limitation

  • Plan excludes specific diagnoses
  • Diagnosis-specific benefit caps reached
  • Condition excluded from covered services
  • Plan limitations on chronic conditions
  • Exhausted diagnosis-specific benefits
⚕️

Medical Necessity

  • Diagnosis doesn't support medical necessity
  • Insufficient clinical justification
  • Treatment not indicated for diagnosis
  • Diagnosis severity insufficient
  • Alternative treatments not attempted
📝

Plan Exclusions

  • Specific diagnoses excluded from coverage
  • Cosmetic/elective procedure diagnoses
  • Pre-existing condition exclusions
  • Diagnosis-specific waiting periods
  • Non-covered condition categories

✅ Step-by-Step Resolution Process

1

Review the Specific Diagnosis Codes Flagged

Examine the ERA/EOB to identify which diagnosis codes were denied as non-covered. Note any remark codes indicating benefit limitations or coverage exclusions.

💡 Pro Tip: CO-167 often pairs with remark codes like N383 (service not covered) or N384 (diagnosis not covered under plan).
2

Check Payer's Covered Diagnosis List

Review the payer's policy manual or contact them to obtain a list of covered diagnoses. Compare the flagged codes against the covered diagnosis ranges to confirm the exclusion.

3

Verify Medical Necessity for the Service

Determine if the submitted diagnosis, while not explicitly excluded, lacks sufficient clinical documentation to establish medical necessity for the services rendered.

⚠️ Important: Some payers may cover a diagnosis if sufficient documentation demonstrates medical necessity, even if it's not explicitly listed as covered.
4

Determine if Alternative Diagnosis Applies

Review medical records for potential alternative diagnoses that may be covered. Consult with the provider to determine if a more specific or different diagnosis code could apply.

5

Submit Appeal with Clinical Documentation

If you believe the denial is incorrect, submit a formal appeal with supporting clinical documentation, medical records, and peer-to-peer review request if appropriate.

6

Consider Patient Financial Responsibility Notification

If the diagnosis is truly non-covered, notify the patient of their financial responsibility. Explore alternative payment options, payment plans, or financial assistance programs.

📞 What to Ask the Payer Representative

Essential Questions for CO-167 Denials:

  1. "Which specific diagnosis codes are not covered under this patient's plan?"
  2. "Is there a covered alternative diagnosis that would apply to this service?"
  3. "What documentation would support an appeal for this denial?"
  4. "Is this a plan-wide exclusion or patient-specific?"
  5. "Does the patient's plan have any exceptions process for non-covered diagnoses?"
  6. "Are there any alternative codes that might be covered for this condition?"
  7. "What is the appeals process and timeline for this type of denial?"
  8. "Can a peer-to-peer review be arranged to discuss medical necessity?"
  9. "Are there any state-mandated coverage requirements that might apply?"
  10. "May I have a reference number for this conversation?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

Reduce CO-167 Denials with These Best Practices:

  • Verify Coverage Before Treatment: Check patient benefits and covered diagnoses prior to providing services
  • Maintain Payer Policy Database: Keep current records of payer-specific diagnosis coverage policies
  • Use Pre-Authorization: Obtain prior authorization for services involving potentially non-covered diagnoses
  • Train Staff on Coverage Rules: Ensure billing staff understand payer-specific diagnosis coverage limitations
  • Implement Eligibility Checks: Verify patient eligibility and benefits before each service
  • Document Medical Necessity: Maintain thorough documentation supporting the medical necessity of treatment
  • Review LCD/NCD Policies: Check Local and National Coverage Determinations for applicable guidelines
  • Monitor Denial Patterns: Track CO-167 denials to identify systemic coverage issues
  • Patient Communication: Inform patients about potential non-covered services before treatment
  • Alternative Diagnosis Review: Consider if a different, covered diagnosis could apply
