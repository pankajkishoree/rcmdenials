CO-109

CO-109 Denial Code: Claim/Service Cannot Be Identified

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Billing

📋 What is CO-109 Denial Code?

CO-109 indicates that the claim or service cannot be identified by the payer's system. the claim may have been submitted with incorrect identifiers.

🔍 Common Causes of CO-109 Denials

❌

Common Causes

  • Incorrect patient identifying information
  • Wrong member ID or subscriber ID
  • Claim submitted to wrong payer
  • Invalid NPI or Tax ID on the claim

✅ How to Resolve CO-109 Denials

1

Review the Denial

Carefully review the remittance advice to understand the specific denial reason.

  • Verify patient demographic information
  • Confirm the correct payer and plan
  • Validate NPI and Tax ID numbers
  • Resubmit with corrected identifying information

📞 What to Ask the Payer

Essential Questions for CO-109 Denials:

  1. "What specific documentation is needed to resolve this denial?"
  2. "What is the appeal deadline for this denial?"
  3. "Can this claim be corrected and resubmitted?"
  4. "Is there a retro-authorization or exception process available?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

  • Verify patient demographics at every visit
  • Confirm insurance eligibility before billing
  • Use clean claim editing software
  • Double-check NPI and Tax ID on every claim
