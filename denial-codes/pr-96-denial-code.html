PR-96

PR-96 Denial Code: Non-Covered Charges

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Patient Responsibility

📋 What is PR-96 Denial Code?

PR-96 is a Patient Responsibility (PR) code indicating that the patient is responsible for charges that are not covered by their insurance plan. These are services deemed non-covered by the payer, meaning the insurance will not pay for them under any circumstances.

PR-96 shifts the financial burden entirely to the patient. It is essential to communicate non-coverage to patients before services are rendered whenever possible, and to discuss payment options and alternatives when applicable.

🔍 Root Causes of PR-96 Denials

🚫

Non-Covered Service

  • Service not included in patient's benefit plan
  • Procedure excluded from coverage under the policy
  • Service not considered medically necessary by payer
  • Treatment type not covered by the specific plan
  • Service rendered at a non-covered facility
💎

Elective/Cosmetic

  • Elective procedures excluded from coverage
  • Cosmetic procedures not covered by insurance
  • Elective surgery without medical necessity
  • Cosmetic enhancements deemed non-essential
  • Voluntary procedures not meeting coverage criteria
🔬

Experimental

  • Service deemed experimental/investigational
  • Treatment not yet approved by FDA or payer
  • Off-label use of device or drug
  • Clinical trial procedures not covered
  • Technology not recognized as standard of care
📊

Plan Limitation

  • Benefit limits exceeded (annual or lifetime)
  • Service exceeds maximum allowed visits/units
  • Coverage exhausted for the benefit period
  • Service not covered during waiting period
  • Out-of-network coverage not included in plan

✅ Step-by-Step Resolution Process

1

Verify Why the Service is Non-Covered

Contact the payer to determine the specific reason the service is non-covered. Ask whether the exclusion is based on the service type, medical necessity, plan limitations, or another reason. This information is critical for determining next steps.

💡 Pro Tip: Request the specific benefit exclusion or plan provision that applies. This helps determine if an appeal is possible or if coverage alternatives exist.
2

Check Patient's Benefit Summary

Review the patient's Summary of Benefits and Coverage (SBC) or Certificate of Coverage to understand the exact plan exclusions and limitations. Compare the billed service against the plan's covered services list.

3

Determine if an Alternative Covered Service Exists

Investigate whether a similar service or alternative treatment is covered under the patient's plan. Sometimes a different procedure code or treatment approach may be covered even when the original service is excluded.

⚠️ Important: Always explore covered alternatives before accepting a non-coverage determination. Patients may have other options that provide similar clinical outcomes.
4

Provide Patient with Advance Notice

When services are determined to be non-covered, provide patients with written notice of non-coverage before services are rendered when possible. This allows patients to make informed decisions about their care and financial responsibility.

5

Discuss Payment Options with Patient

Meet with the patient to discuss their financial responsibility for the non-covered charges. Offer payment plans, payment assistance programs, or financial counseling services to help patients manage the out-of-pocket costs.

6

File Appeal if Coverage Should Apply

If you believe the service should be covered based on medical necessity, plan benefits, or other criteria, file a formal appeal with the payer. Include clinical documentation, medical necessity justification, and any relevant plan provisions.

📞 What to Ask the Payer Representative

Essential Questions for PR-96 Denials:

  1. "Why is this specific service non-covered under the patient's plan?"
  2. "Is there a covered alternative service or procedure that would achieve the same clinical outcome?"
  3. "What is the patient's estimated financial responsibility for this service?"
  4. "Is an appeal available for this non-coverage determination?"
  5. "What is the appeals process and timeline for this determination?"
  6. "Does the patient's plan have any exceptions process for non-covered services?"
  7. "Can you provide the specific plan provision or exclusion that applies?"
  8. "Is there a network or facility restriction that caused this non-coverage?"
  9. "Are there any state-mandated coverage requirements that might apply?"
  10. "May I have a reference number for this conversation?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

Reduce PR-96 Denials with These Best Practices:

  • Verify Coverage Before Service: Check patient benefits and coverage for the specific service before delivery
  • Obtain Pre-Service Determination: Request a pre-determination of benefits for services that may have coverage questions
  • Communicate Non-Coverage Early: Inform patients of non-coverage in writing before services are rendered when possible
  • Document Patient Acknowledgment: Have patients sign financial responsibility forms acknowledging potential non-coverage
  • Explore Covered Alternatives: Research alternative treatments or procedure codes that may be covered
  • Know Plan Exclusions: Familiarize yourself with common exclusions for each payer and plan type
  • Use Eligibility Verification: Run real-time eligibility checks to confirm coverage status
  • Train Front Desk Staff: Ensure scheduling staff verify coverage before booking services
  • Maintain Payer Contacts: Keep direct contacts with payer representatives for coverage questions
  • Audit Non-Coverage Patterns: Track non-coverage denials to identify systemic issues or opportunities for process improvement
