CO-23

CO-23 Denial Code: Payment Adjusted Due to Interest Penalty Clause

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Contractual Obligation

📋 What is CO-23 Denial Code?

CO-23 is a Contractual Obligation (CO) denial code indicating that the payment has been adjusted because the claim was not submitted within the payer's timely filing limit, resulting in an interest penalty or reduced payment. This denial occurs when claims miss contractual filing deadlines.

The payer applies an interest penalty or reduces payment when claims are filed beyond the agreed-upon timely filing window. This adjustment is based on the contractual terms between the provider and the payer.

🔍 Root Causes of CO-23 Denials

📅

Late Claim Submission

  • Claim submitted after payer's timely filing deadline
  • Missed filing deadline due to miscalculated dates
  • Claims held in queues past submission deadlines
  • Delayed posting by clearinghouse or payer
  • Missed re-file deadlines on previously denied claims
⏳

Processing Delays

  • Internal delays in claim preparation or submission
  • Staffing shortages causing claim backlog
  • Pending provider authentication or enrollment
  • Delayed charge capture from facility systems
  • Manual review processes causing submission lag
🖥️

Clearinghouse Errors

  • Claim rejected or held by clearinghouse past filing deadline
  • Clearinghouse edits not resolved in time
  • Electronic transmission failures not caught promptly
  • Payer connectivity issues delaying submission
  • Incorrect payer routing causing rejections
🔄

Missing Follow-Up

  • Previously denied claim not resubmitted within allowed timeframe
  • Appeal deadlines missed for initial denials
  • Lack of systematic claim tracking process
  • No automated alerts for approaching deadlines
  • Insufficient resources for timely filing management

✅ Step-by-Step Resolution Process

1

Determine Filing Dates

Determine the original submission date and the payer's filing deadline. Review the ERA/EOB for the specific timely filing deadline that was missed and calculate the number of days past the deadline.

💡 Pro Tip: Request the payer's timely filing policy in writing to establish the exact deadline requirements for future claims.
2

Obtain Proof of Submission

Obtain proof of original submission such as clearinghouse reports, certified mail receipts, or fax confirmation. Gather documentation that establishes when the claim was actually submitted to the payer.

3

Verify Appeal Window

Verify if the denial is within the allowed appeal window. Check the payer's appeal deadline and ensure you have sufficient time to prepare and submit the appeal with supporting documentation.

4

Gather Exception Documentation

Gather supporting documentation for timely filing exception. Collect any evidence that justifies the late submission, such as system outages, natural disasters, or payer processing errors.

⚠️ Important: Document every step of the submission timeline. Payers may accept exceptions for circumstances beyond the provider's control.
5

Submit Appeal

Submit appeal with proof of timely submission or request exception. Include all supporting documentation, a detailed timeline of events, and a clear explanation for why the claim was submitted late.

6

Follow Up on Appeal

Follow up within 30 days of appeal submission. Track the appeal status regularly and be prepared to provide additional documentation if requested by the payer.

📞 What to Ask the Payer Representative

Essential Questions for CO-23 Denials:

  1. "What is the exact filing deadline that was missed?"
  2. "What is the original date of service and date the claim was received?"
  3. "Does the payer accept clearinghouse timestamp as proof of timely filing?"
  4. "Is there an exception process for timely filing denials?"
  5. "What documentation is required to overturn this denial?"
  6. "Is the interest penalty amount negotiable based on circumstances?"
  7. "Can you provide the timely filing policy in writing?"
  8. "What is the appeal deadline for this denial?"
  9. "Are there specific circumstances that qualify for a timely filing exception?"
  10. "May I have a reference number for this conversation?"

⚖️ Appeal Guidance

If you believe this denial was made in error, you have the right to appeal. Follow these steps:

  1. Review the denial thoroughly — Understand exactly why the claim was denied.
  2. Gather supporting evidence — Collect all medical records and billing documentation.
  3. Write a detailed appeal letter — Address each denial reason specifically.
  4. Submit within the deadline — Most payers require appeals within 30-180 days.
  5. Follow up regularly — Track your appeal status.

Essential Questions for Your Appeal:

  • "What specific documentation is needed to resolve this denial?"
  • "What is the appeal deadline for this denial?"
  • "Can this claim be corrected and resubmitted?"
  • "Is there a peer-to-peer review option available?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

Reduce CO-23 Denials with These Best Practices:

  • Track Filing Deadlines: Maintain a centralized system to track all payer-specific timely filing deadlines
  • Automate Submission Alerts: Set up automated notifications for claims approaching filing deadlines
  • Submit Claims Promptly: Establish a policy to submit claims within 48-72 hours of service
  • Monitor Clearinghouse Status: Check clearinghouse reports daily for rejected or held claims
  • Implement Claim Tracking: Use practice management software to track claim status and deadlines
  • Document Everything: Keep records of all submission confirmations, timestamps, and correspondence
  • Train Staff on Deadlines: Ensure all billing staff understand payer-specific filing requirements
  • Establish Escalation Procedures: Create protocols for claims approaching deadline without resolution
  • Review Payer Contracts: Maintain current copies of all payer contracts with filing requirements
  • Conduct Regular Audits: Periodically review claim submission timelines to identify bottlenecks
