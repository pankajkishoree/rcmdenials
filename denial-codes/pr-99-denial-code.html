PR-99

PR-99 Denial Code: Benefit Unknown

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Patient Responsibility

📋 What is PR-99 Denial Code?

PR-99 indicates that the patient's benefit status could not be determined. This may require eligibility verification and reprocessing of the claim. The payer is unable to determine the patient's benefits based on the information provided.

This code typically results from eligibility issues, plan changes, or data entry errors. Resolving it requires verifying patient information and resubmitting with corrected details.

🔍 Root Causes

Common Reasons for PR-99:

  • Eligibility Not Verified: Patient eligibility not confirmed prior to service
  • Plan Changes: Patient's plan changed and records not updated
  • Data Entry Error: Incorrect member information submitted
  • System Error: Payer system unable to determine benefits

✅ How to Handle PR-99

1

Verify Patient Eligibility for Date of Service

Confirm the patient's eligibility status for the specific date of service.

  • Check eligibility through payer portal or clearinghouse
  • Verify effective dates of coverage
  • Confirm the patient was eligible on the date of service
  • Document eligibility verification results
2

Confirm Member ID and Demographic Information

Verify all patient demographic and insurance information is accurate.

💡 Verification Checklist:
  • Correct member ID number
  • Accurate date of birth
  • Correct subscriber name
  • Valid group number (if applicable)
  • Correct payer and plan information
3

Check for Recent Plan Changes

Determine if the patient's insurance plan has changed recently.

  • Ask patient about any recent coverage changes
  • Check for open enrollment transitions
  • Verify if employer or group coverage changed
  • Document any plan changes identified
4

Request Updated Eligibility from Payer

Contact the payer to obtain current eligibility information.

  • Call payer eligibility department
  • Request real-time eligibility verification
  • Obtain written confirmation of benefits
  • Document payer representative and reference number
5

Resubmit Claim with Corrected Information

Submit a corrected claim with accurate eligibility information.

  • Update all demographic and insurance data
  • Add eligibility verification notes
  • Include supporting documentation
  • Submit as a corrected or replacement claim
6

Follow Up to Confirm Processing

Monitor the resubmitted claim to ensure proper processing.

Follow-Up Actions:
  • Track claim status weekly
  • Confirm receipt of corrected information
  • Verify benefits are now determinable
  • Document final claim outcome
  • Update patient if liability changes

📞 What to Ask the Payer

Essential Questions for PR-99:

  1. "Can you verify the patient's current eligibility?"
  2. "Has there been any recent plan change?"
  3. "What information is needed to determine benefits?"
  4. "Is the member ID correct in your system?"
  5. "Can you provide a real-time eligibility verification?"
  6. "What is the correct payer ID and plan information?"
  7. "Is there a secondary insurance on file?"

💡 Best Practices for Eligibility Verification

  • Pre-Service Verification: Always verify eligibility before providing service
  • Real-Time Checks: Use real-time eligibility verification tools
  • Automated Systems: Implement automated eligibility checking
  • Staff Training: Train staff on proper verification procedures
  • Patient Reminders: Ask patients to bring updated insurance cards
  • Regular Audits: Audit eligibility verification compliance
  • Clear Documentation: Record all verification attempts and results
  • Payer Portals: Utilize payer portals for instant verification
  • Batch Processing: Verify eligibility for scheduled patients in advance
  • Follow-Up Protocol: Establish protocol for handling eligibility issues
