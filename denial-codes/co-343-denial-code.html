CO-343

CO-343 Denial Code: Services Not Authorized by the Managed Care Organization

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Authorization

📋 What is CO-343 Denial Code?

CO-343 is a Contractual Obligation (CO) denial code indicating that payment was adjusted because the services were not authorized by the managed care organization (MCO) as required by the patient's plan. This denial occurs when a required prior authorization was not obtained or linked to the claim before services were rendered.

MCO plans typically require prior authorization for specific services, procedures, or settings. Failure to obtain proper authorization before delivering care results in the payer denying payment for those services.

🔍 Root Causes of CO-343 Denials

🚫

No Authorization Obtained

  • Required prior authorization was not obtained before service
  • Authorization request never submitted to MCO
  • Staff unaware authorization was required
  • Emergency case with no retro-auth obtained
  • Authorization exemption not applicable
⏰

Authorization Expired

  • Authorization expired before the date of service
  • Service not completed within auth validity window
  • Auth period did not cover the treatment duration
  • Delayed service delivery past auth deadline
  • Recertification not obtained for ongoing care
🔄

Wrong Service Authed

  • Authorization does not cover the service provided
  • Auth obtained for different procedure code
  • Auth covers evaluation but not the treatment rendered
  • Modifier on claim differs from authorized service
  • Auth obtained for wrong body part or diagnosis
📋

MCO Requirement

  • MCO-specific authorization rules not followed
  • Required MCO referral not obtained
  • Authorization not linked to claim at submission
  • MCO form or process not properly completed
  • Network status requirements not met

✅ Step-by-Step Resolution Process

1

Check if Authorization Was Required

Review the patient's MCO plan benefits and the specific service billed to determine if prior authorization was required. Check the payer's authorization requirements for the CPT/HCPCS code and place of service.

💡 Pro Tip: Authorization requirements vary by MCO plan. Always verify requirements specific to the patient's plan, not just the payer in general.
2

Verify Existing Authorization

Check whether an authorization was obtained but simply not linked to the claim. Search the MCO portal, contact the authorization department, or call the payer to verify if an auth number exists for this patient and service date.

3

Determine Retro-Authorization Availability

Contact the MCO to determine if retro-authorization is available. Some plans allow retro-authorization within a specific window (typically 24-72 hours for emergencies, or up to 30 days for urgent situations).

⚠️ Important: Retro-authorization is not always available. Check the specific MCO policy and act quickly if it is an option.
4

Gather Clinical Documentation

Collect all clinical documentation supporting the medical necessity of the services provided. This includes progress notes, lab results, imaging reports, and any documentation that justifies why the service was medically necessary.

5

Submit Appeal with Authorization Request

File a formal appeal with the MCO. Include the authorization request, clinical documentation supporting medical necessity, and any evidence that the service should be covered under the patient's plan benefits.

6

Establish Authorization Processes

Implement or improve authorization tracking processes for future claims. Create checklists, assign authorization responsibilities, and verify MCO requirements before delivering services to prevent similar denials.

📞 What to Ask the Payer Representative

Essential Questions for CO-343 Denials:

  1. "Was prior authorization required for this specific service under the patient's MCO plan?"
  2. "Is retro-authorization available, and if so, what is the deadline to submit?"
  3. "What specific documentation is needed to support an authorization request or appeal?"
  4. "What is the authorization deadline for this type of service?"
  5. "Can you confirm the authorization requirements for this CPT code under this plan?"
  6. "Is there an authorization number already on file for this patient and date of service?"
  7. "What is the appeal process and timeline for this denial?"
  8. "Are there any MCO-specific forms required for the authorization request?"
  9. "What is the timely filing deadline for submitting the appeal?"
  10. "May I have a reference number for this conversation?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

Reduce CO-343 Denials with These Best Practices:

  • Verify Authorization Requirements: Check MCO authorization requirements for every patient before delivering services
  • Implement Authorization Tracking: Use a centralized system to track all pending, approved, and expired authorizations
  • Train Front Desk Staff: Ensure scheduling staff verify authorization requirements at patient check-in
  • Submit Authorizations Early: Submit authorization requests well in advance of the planned service date
  • Monitor Auth Expiration: Set alerts for upcoming authorization expirations to request extensions or recertifications
  • Link Auth to Claims: Always include the authorization number on the claim at submission
  • Document Auth Conversations: Keep records of all authorization-related communications with the MCO
  • Know Emergency Rules: Understand MCO policies for emergency services and retro-authorization windows
  • Use Payer Portals: Verify authorization status through MCO online portals before claim submission
  • Audit Authorization Compliance: Conduct regular audits to ensure authorization processes are being followed
