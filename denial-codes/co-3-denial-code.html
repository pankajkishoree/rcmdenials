CO-3

CO-3 Denial Code: Coinsurance Amount

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Patient Responsibility

📋 What is CO-3 Denial Code?

CO-3 indicates that the coinsurance amount has been applied. the patient is responsible for a percentage of the allowed amount.

🔍 Common Causes of CO-3 Denials

❌

Common Causes

  • Patient plan requires coinsurance payment (e.g., 20% of allowed amount)
  • Coinsurance applies after deductible is met
  • Different coinsurance rates for in-network vs out-of-network
  • Service-specific coinsurance rules apply

✅ How to Resolve CO-3 Denials

1

Review the Denial

Carefully review the remittance advice to understand the specific denial reason.

  • Calculate the correct coinsurance percentage per the patient's plan
  • Apply patient responsibility for the coinsurance amount
  • Verify in-network vs out-of-network coinsurance rates
  • If coinsurance calculation is wrong, appeal with plan documents

📞 What to Ask the Payer

Essential Questions for CO-3 Denials:

  1. "What specific documentation is needed to resolve this denial?"
  2. "What is the appeal deadline for this denial?"
  3. "Can this claim be corrected and resubmitted?"
  4. "Is there a retro-authorization or exception process available?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

  • Verify coinsurance percentages before claim submission
  • Track in-network vs out-of-network rates for each patient
  • Apply correct coinsurance based on service type
  • Educate patients on their coinsurance responsibility
