PR-3

PR-3 Denial Code: Copayment Amount

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Patient Responsibility

📋 What is PR-3 Denial Code?

PR-3 is a Patient Responsibility (PR) code indicating that the patient is responsible for a copayment amount. A copayment (or copay) is a fixed dollar amount that a patient must pay at the time of service for a specific type of visit or service, as defined by their health insurance plan.

Unlike coinsurance which is a percentage, copayments are set dollar amounts (e.g., $25 for office visits, $50 for specialists). This code represents the patient's responsibility that should be collected according to the provider's financial policy.

🔍 Root Causes of PR-3 Balances

🏥

Office Visit Copay

  • Standard primary care office visit copay
  • Preventive care copay (if applicable)
  • Telehealth visit copay
  • Follow-up visit copay
  • Annual physical copay (if not fully covered)
👨‍⚕️

Specialist Copay

  • Higher copay for specialist visits
  • Consultation copay for new patient specialist
  • Subspecialist referral copay
  • Surgical consultation copay
  • Behavioral health specialist copay
🚑

Urgent Care Copay

  • Higher copay for urgent care visits
  • After-hours care copay
  • Urgent care vs. ER copay differential
  • Retail clinic copay for minor conditions
  • Walk-in clinic copay
🚨

Emergency Room Copay

  • ER visit copay (often highest tier)
  • ER copay waiver if admitted
  • Facility vs. professional ER copay
  • Observation status ER copay
  • Emergency department copay by facility type

✅ Step-by-Step Resolution Process

1

Verify the Copay Amount

Check the EOB/ERA to confirm the copay amount applied matches the patient's benefit plan. Copay amounts are fixed and should not vary based on the total charges.

💡 Pro Tip: Copay amounts are typically listed in the patient's benefit summary. Compare the applied copay to the plan document to ensure accuracy.
2

Review Visit Type Classification

Verify the payer correctly classified the visit type (office visit, specialist, urgent care, ER). Misclassification can result in the wrong copay amount being applied.

3

Check for Waived Copays

Determine if the copay should have been waived for preventive services or other specific scenarios. Some plans waive copays for annual wellness visits or certain preventive screenings.

⚠️ Important: If the patient already paid the copay at check-in, verify the payer did not also deduct it from the claim payment, resulting in double collection.
4

Bill the Patient

If the copay was not collected at the time of service, send a patient statement for the outstanding copay amount. Reference the PR-3 code and include the EOB for transparency.

5

Follow Up on Unpaid Copays

Monitor and follow up on unpaid patient copay balances. Implement a consistent collection process to ensure copays are collected within your practice's financial policy guidelines.

📞 What to Ask the Payer Representative

Essential Questions for PR-3 Balances:

  1. "What copay amount applies for this type of visit under this patient's plan?"
  2. "How did the payer classify this visit type for copay purposes?"
  3. "Is the copay waived for preventive services under this plan?"
  4. "Has the patient already paid the copay, and was it properly applied?"
  5. "Does this plan have a copay accumulator that affects the out-of-pocket maximum?"
  6. "Are there different copay tiers for different provider types?"
  7. "Does the copay apply per visit or per date of service?"
  8. "Is a copay required for telehealth services under this plan?"
  9. "What is the patient's current deductible and OOP maximum status?"
  10. "May I have a reference number for this conversation?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

Reduce PR-3 Collection Challenges with These Best Practices:

  • Collect Copays at Check-In: Make copay collection a standard front-desk procedure
  • Post Copay Signs: Display signs in the waiting area reminding patients of copay requirements
  • Train Front Desk Staff: Ensure staff ask for copay at every visit and know how to verify amounts
  • Verify Copay Amounts: Check real-time benefit verification for accurate copay amounts
  • Offer Payment Options: Accept credit cards, mobile payments, and online payments for copays
  • Track Copay Collections: Monitor point-of-service collection rates and address low performance
  • Send Reminders: Include copay information in appointment reminder messages
  • Have a Financial Policy: Clearly communicate copay expectations during patient registration
  • Reconcile Copays with ERA: Match collected copays with the EOB to ensure accuracy
  • Follow Up on Unpaid Copays: Send timely statements for copays not collected at time of service
