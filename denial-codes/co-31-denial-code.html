CO-31

CO-31 Denial Code: Patient Cannot Be Identified as Our Insured

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Eligibility

📋 What is CO-31 Denial Code?

CO-31 is a Contractual Obligation (CO) denial code indicating that the patient cannot be identified as an insured member of the payer's plan. This may be due to incorrect member ID, name mismatch, or the patient not being enrolled in the plan.

The payer requires verification that the patient is an active, enrolled member with valid coverage on the date of service. Without proper identification, the payer cannot process the claim against the patient's benefits.

🔍 Root Causes of CO-31 Denials

🔢

Incorrect Member ID

  • Wrong insurance ID number submitted on the claim
  • Transposed digits in the member ID field
  • Using an expired or inactive member ID
  • Submitting a dependent's ID for subscriber claims
  • Missing prefix or suffix on the member ID
⚠️

Name Mismatch

  • Patient name on claim doesn't match payer's records
  • Typographical errors in patient name
  • Legal name change not updated with payer
  • Missing middle name or initial
  • Name order discrepancy (first/last swapped)
🔄

Coverage Termination

  • Patient's coverage ended before the date of service
  • Employer terminated coverage without patient knowledge
  • Premium payment lapse causing coverage gap
  • Plan year renewal not processed timely
  • Cobra coverage expired
📋

Enrollment Issues

  • Patient not properly enrolled in the plan
  • New enrollment pending activation
  • Plan change not reflected in payer system
  • Waiting period not yet satisfied
  • Eligibility data not transmitted to payer

✅ Step-by-Step Resolution Process

1

Verify Patient Identification

Verify the member ID, name, DOB, and gender submitted on the claim. Compare these details against the patient's insurance card and any available eligibility information to identify discrepancies.

💡 Pro Tip: Always request a copy of the patient's current insurance card during registration to avoid identification errors at the payer level.
2

Request Updated Eligibility Information

Contact the payer to request updated eligibility information for the patient. Confirm the correct member ID, effective dates, and current coverage status. Document all information received from the payer representative.

3

Confirm Active Coverage

Confirm if the patient has active coverage on the date of service. Check for any coverage gaps, effective dates, and termination dates that may affect claim adjudication.

⚠️ Important: Coverage verification must be performed for the exact date of service billed. Active coverage today does not guarantee coverage on the service date.
4

Check for Recent Plan Changes

Check for any recent plan changes or coverage transitions. Patients may have switched employers, plans, or coverage tiers that affect eligibility. Verify if the patient has primary or secondary coverage.

5

Resubmit with Corrected Information

Resubmit with corrected patient information if errors were found. Use frequency code 7 (replacement claim) and include the original claim number. Clearly indicate the corrected member ID or patient demographics.

6

Determine Alternative Coverage

If patient is not covered under the billed insurance, determine if another payer should be billed. Check for other active insurance policies, coordination of benefits, or secondary coverage that may apply.

📞 What to Ask the Payer Representative

Essential Questions for CO-31 Denials:

  1. "Can you confirm the member ID and name on file for this patient?"
  2. "Was the patient eligible on the date of service?"
  3. "Has there been any recent change to the patient's coverage?"
  4. "Is there a different insurance ID this patient may be covered under?"
  5. "What specific field caused the identification failure?"
  6. "Are there any remarks or additional denial details beyond CO-31?"
  7. "Does the payer have updated eligibility information in their system?"
  8. "What is the correct member ID format for this plan?"
  9. "Is the patient listed as subscriber or dependent on the plan?"
  10. "May I have a reference number for this conversation?"

⚖️ Appeal Guidance

If you believe this denial was made in error, you have the right to appeal. Follow these steps:

  1. Review the denial thoroughly — Understand exactly why the claim was denied.
  2. Gather supporting evidence — Collect all medical records and billing documentation.
  3. Write a detailed appeal letter — Address each denial reason specifically.
  4. Submit within the deadline — Most payers require appeals within 30-180 days.
  5. Follow up regularly — Track your appeal status.

Essential Questions for Your Appeal:

  • "What specific documentation is needed to resolve this denial?"
  • "What is the appeal deadline for this denial?"
  • "Can this claim be corrected and resubmitted?"
  • "Is there a peer-to-peer review option available?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

Reduce CO-31 Denials with These Best Practices:

  • Verify Eligibility at Registration: Run real-time eligibility checks before every patient visit to confirm active coverage
  • Collect Current Insurance Cards: Request updated insurance cards at each visit and scan both sides
  • Validate Member ID Format: Use claim scrubbers to verify member ID format and checksum before submission
  • Confirm Patient Demographics: Verify name spelling, DOB, and gender against insurance records during check-in
  • Check Coverage Effective Dates: Confirm coverage is active for the date of service, not just current status
  • Monitor Enrollment Changes: Flag new patients and those with recent plan changes for additional verification
  • Implement Batch Eligibility Checks: Run nightly batch eligibility verification for upcoming appointments
  • Train Front Desk Staff: Ensure registration staff understand importance of accurate insurance data entry
  • Use Electronic Verification: Utilize clearinghouse eligibility tools for instant coverage confirmation
  • Maintain Insurance Matrix: Keep an updated reference of payer member ID formats and requirements
