OA-18

OA-18 Denial Code: Primary/Secondary Payment Coordination

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Other Adjustment

📋 What is OA-18 Denial Code?

OA-18 is an Other Adjustment (OA) code indicating that the claim was paid by the primary payer and this represents the secondary payment adjustment. This code reflects the coordination of benefits (COB) between primary and secondary insurance plans.

OA-18 typically appears on secondary payer remittance advices to show how the secondary benefit was calculated after the primary payment was applied. It is often informational rather than a true denial, indicating the secondary allowed amount and any remaining patient responsibility.

🔍 Root Causes of OA-18 Adjustments

🔄

COB Adjustment

  • Primary payment subtracted from allowed amount
  • Standard coordination of benefits calculation
  • Secondary payer reduces payment by primary amount
  • Non-duplication of benefits rule applied
  • Primary payment exceeds secondary allowance
💰

Primary Payment Applied

  • Primary paid claim before secondary processed
  • Primary payment amount integrated into COB
  • Secondary payer adjusts to avoid overpayment
  • Primary contractual adjustment reflected
  • Primary deductible and copay amounts included
📐

Secondary Benefits Calculated

  • Secondary allowed amount minus primary payment
  • Lesser of primary payment or secondary allowance
  • Secondary deductible applied after primary credit
  • Secondary coinsurance calculated on remaining balance
  • Out-of-pocket maximum credit from primary
👤

Patient Responsibility Transferred

  • Remaining balance after both insurances applied
  • Patient deductible not met on secondary plan
  • Secondary copay or coinsurance remaining
  • Non-covered charges after both payments
  • Remaining patient balance transferred to provider

✅ Step-by-Step Resolution Process

1

Review Primary and Secondary EOBs

Compare the primary and secondary remittance advices to understand how the COB calculation was performed. Verify that the primary payment was correctly reported and applied.

💡 Pro Tip: Ensure the primary EOB was submitted with the secondary claim. Many secondary denials occur because the primary EOB was not attached or was incomplete.
2

Verify Coordination of Benefits

Confirm the correct primary and secondary payer order according to COB rules. Check that the correct insurance was designated as primary based on birthday rule, active employment status, or Medicare entitlement.

3

Check Secondary Payer Calculation

Review the secondary payer's calculation methodology. The secondary typically pays the lesser of its allowed amount minus the primary payment, or its full allowed amount minus its own deductible/coinsurance.

⚠️ Important: If the primary payment equals or exceeds the secondary allowed amount, the secondary may pay $0. This is correct COB and not an error.
4

Bill Remaining Patient Balance

After both primary and secondary payments are applied, the remaining balance (if any) becomes the patient's responsibility per the secondary plan's benefits. Bill the patient for any remaining amounts.

5

Follow Up on Remaining Balance

Track the patient balance after COB and follow up according to your financial policy. If the secondary payment was incorrect, file an appeal with the secondary payer providing all primary claim documentation.

📞 What to Ask the Payer Representative

Essential Questions for OA-18 Adjustments:

  1. "What was the primary payment amount used in the COB calculation?"
  2. "How was the secondary benefit calculated for this claim?"
  3. "What is the secondary allowed amount before COB adjustment?"
  4. "Does the secondary plan use the 'lesser of' or 'standard' COB method?"
  5. "Was the primary EOB received and processed correctly?"
  6. "Is the remaining balance patient responsibility, or should it be written off?"
  7. "What is the patient's remaining deductible and OOP maximum on the secondary plan?"
  8. "Are there any COB-related appeals or reconsideration options available?"
  9. "Can you provide a detailed breakdown of the COB calculation?"
  10. "May I have a reference number for this conversation?"

⚖️ Appeal Guidance

If you believe this denial was made in error, you have the right to appeal. Follow these steps:

  1. Review the denial thoroughly — Understand exactly why the claim was denied.
  2. Gather supporting evidence — Collect all medical records and billing documentation.
  3. Write a detailed appeal letter — Address each denial reason specifically.
  4. Submit within the deadline — Most payers require appeals within 30-180 days.
  5. Follow up regularly — Track your appeal status.

Essential Questions for Your Appeal:

  • "What specific documentation is needed to resolve this denial?"
  • "What is the appeal deadline for this denial?"
  • "Can this claim be corrected and resubmitted?"
  • "Is there a peer-to-peer review option available?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

Reduce OA-18 Adjustment Issues with These Best Practices:

  • Verify COB at Registration: Collect complete primary and secondary insurance information at patient check-in
  • Confirm Primary Payer Order: Use COB rules (birthday rule, active employment) to verify correct payer order
  • Include Primary EOB: Always attach the primary EOB when filing secondary claims
  • Use Correct COB Codes: Ensure the correct coordination of benefits codes are on the secondary claim
  • File Secondary Timely: Submit secondary claims promptly after receiving primary payment determination
  • Verify Primary Payment Accuracy: Review primary EOBs for correctness before submitting to secondary
  • Educate Patients on COB: Inform patients with multiple insurances about proper COB procedures
  • Track Dual Coverage Patients: Maintain a registry of patients with primary and secondary coverage
  • Monitor Secondary Payments: Review secondary EOBs to ensure correct COB calculations
  • Document COB Communications: Record all payer interactions regarding coordination of benefits
