CO-252

CO-252 Denial Code: An Attachment/Other Document Is Required

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Documentation

📋 What is CO-252 Denial Code?

CO-252 is a Contractual Obligation (CO) denial code indicating that payment is withheld because an attachment or additional documentation is required to process the claim. The payer needs supporting documents before adjudication.

This denial occurs when the claim is incomplete without the necessary supporting documentation. The payer cannot evaluate medical necessity or coverage without the required attachments.

🔍 Root Causes of CO-252 Denials

📄

Missing Medical Records

  • Clinical documentation not submitted
  • Progress notes missing from claim
  • Lab or imaging results not attached
  • Treatment history not included
  • Operative reports missing
🔑

Authorization Not Attached

  • Prior authorization documentation missing
  • Authorization number not included on claim
  • Auth approval letter not submitted
  • Extension or modification not documented
  • Auth for different service than billed
🔄

Referral Missing

  • PCP referral documentation required
  • Referral form not submitted with claim
  • Specialist referral expired
  • Referral for wrong specialty or service
  • Internal referral not documented
📋

Specific Payer Requirement

  • Payer requires additional forms or letters
  • Custom payer documentation templates
  • Payer-specific attachment checklists
  • State-mandated documentation
  • Plan-specific forms required

✅ Step-by-Step Resolution Process

1

Identify What Specific Attachment Is Required

Review the ERA/EOB carefully to determine exactly which document or attachment the payer needs. Check for specific remark codes that indicate the type of documentation required (e.g., medical records, auth letter, referral form).

💡 Pro Tip: Common remark codes with CO-252 include N370 (documentation needed) and N575 (prior authorization required). Always cross-reference these with payer-specific guidelines.
2

Gather the Required Documentation

Collect all documents the payer has requested. This may include medical records, prior authorization letters, referral forms, clinical notes, or other supporting documentation. Ensure all documents are complete and legible.

3

Submit Attachment via Payer Portal, Fax, or Mail

Send the required documentation using the payer's preferred submission method. Most payers accept electronic uploads through their provider portal, fax, or physical mail. Always use the payer's designated attachment submission channel.

4

Include the Claim Number on All Attachments

Clearly mark every page of your submission with the original claim number, patient name, member ID, and date of service. This ensures the payer can match your documentation to the correct claim.

⚠️ Important: Unidentified attachments may be lost or misfiled, causing further delays. Always include identifying information on every page.
5

Confirm Receipt with Payer

Contact the payer to verify they received your documentation. Request a confirmation number or reference for the submitted attachment. This serves as proof of submission if the claim is denied again.

6

Follow Up Within 14 Days

Track the claim after submitting documentation. Follow up within 14 days to ensure the payer is processing the claim with the newly submitted attachments. Document all communication and keep records of submission confirmations.

📞 What to Ask the Payer Representative

Essential Questions for CO-252 Denials:

  1. "What specific attachment is required for this claim?"
  2. "What is the deadline to submit the required documentation?"
  3. "Can I submit the attachment electronically through your portal?"
  4. "How do I confirm receipt of my submitted documentation?"
  5. "Are there specific forms or templates I need to use?"
  6. "Is there a fax number or mailing address for attachment submissions?"
  7. "What remark codes are associated with this denial?"
  8. "Will processing resume once the attachment is received, or is a new claim needed?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

Reduce CO-252 Denials with These Best Practices:

  • Maintain Attachment Checklists: Create payer-specific checklists of required documentation for common services
  • Verify Requirements Before Submission: Check payer guidelines for attachment requirements before filing claims
  • Use Pre-Submission Audits: Implement claim scrubbers that flag missing attachments before submission
  • Track Authorization Status: Maintain a system to ensure auth documentation is attached to claims
  • Document Referrals Properly: Ensure referral forms are completed and filed before services are rendered
  • Train Staff on Payer Requirements: Educate billing staff on payer-specific attachment policies
  • Implement Digital Document Management: Use electronic systems to organize and retrieve required documents quickly
  • Verify Before Filing: Double-check all required attachments are included before claim submission
  • Maintain Medical Records Access: Ensure quick access to clinical documentation for attachment requests
  • Monitor Denial Trends: Track attachment-related denials to identify patterns and improve processes
