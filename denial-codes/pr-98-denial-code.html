PR-98

PR-98 Denial Code: Claim/Service Denied — Patient Liability

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Patient Responsibility

📋 What is PR-98 Denial Code?

PR-98 indicates that the patient is liable for this amount because the claim/service has been denied and the patient is responsible for the full or partial charges. This code applies when insurance will not pay and the financial responsibility shifts to the patient.

Proper handling of PR-98 requires determining the root cause of denial, assessing appeal potential, and communicating clearly with the patient about their financial obligations.

🔍 Root Causes

Common Reasons for PR-98:

  • Service Denied: Claim denied and patient responsible for charges
  • Benefit Exhaustion: Patient's benefits fully utilized
  • Non-Covered Service: Service not covered under patient's plan, patient pays
  • Coordination of Benefits: Patient responsible after primary denial

✅ How to Handle PR-98

1

Determine Why the Claim Was Denied

Identify the exact reason for the denial to understand patient liability.

  • Review the denial reason code on the remittance advice
  • Check if the denial was for medical necessity
  • Determine if it was a coverage or eligibility issue
  • Document the denial reason for patient communication
2

Check if the Denial Can Be Appealed

Assess whether an appeal is warranted before shifting costs to the patient.

💡 Appeal Considerations:
  • Review appeal deadlines and requirements
  • Gather supporting clinical documentation
  • Determine if medical necessity can be demonstrated
  • Assess cost-benefit of pursuing the appeal
3

Verify Patient's Benefit Status

Confirm the patient's current benefit status and coverage details.

  • Check remaining benefits and coverage limits
  • Verify if benefits are exhausted for the year
  • Confirm any remaining out-of-pocket maximums
  • Document benefit status for accurate billing
4

Calculate Accurate Patient Responsibility

Determine the exact amount the patient owes based on the denial.

  • Review original charges billed
  • Apply any contractual adjustments
  • Deduct any payments already received
  • Calculate final patient balance
5

Bill Patient with Clear Explanation

Send a detailed statement explaining the denial and patient responsibility.

  • Include denial reason in patient communication
  • Provide itemized statement of charges
  • Explain insurance processing in plain language
  • Include payment options and deadlines
6

Offer Payment Plan Options

Provide flexible payment solutions for patients facing significant liability.

Payment Solutions:
  • Interest-free installment plans
  • Credit card on file programs
  • Online payment portal access
  • Financial assistance screening
  • Hardship considerations when applicable

📞 What to Ask the Payer

Essential Questions for PR-98:

  1. "Why was the claim denied?"
  2. "What is the exact patient responsibility amount?"
  3. "Can this denial be appealed?"
  4. "What are the patient's payment options?"
  5. "Is there any remaining insurance coverage for this service?"
  6. "What documentation is needed for an appeal?"
  7. "Are there any secondary insurance considerations?"

📎 Required Documentation for Appeal

  • Original claim with all codes and modifiers
  • Complete medical records supporting medical necessity
  • Any prior authorization or precertification documentation
  • Provider-payer contract or fee schedule reference
  • Detailed appeal letter with clinical justification

💡 Best Practices for Patient Liability Claims

  • Pre-Service Verification: Check coverage before providing service
  • Advance Beneficiary Notice: Issue ABN for services likely to be denied
  • Financial Counseling: Inform patients of potential liability upfront
  • Clear Communication: Explain patient responsibility in plain language
  • Timely Billing: Send patient statements promptly after denial
  • Payment Plans: Offer manageable payment options
  • Documentation: Maintain thorough records of all communications
  • Appeal When Warranted: Don't shift costs without reviewing appeal options
  • Financial Assistance: Screen patients for assistance programs
  • Consistent Follow-Up: Track patient payment status regularly

⚖️ Appeal Guidance

If you believe this denial was made in error, you have the right to appeal. Follow these steps:

  1. Review the denial thoroughly — Understand exactly why the claim was denied.
  2. Gather supporting evidence — Collect all medical records and billing documentation.
  3. Write a detailed appeal letter — Address each denial reason specifically.
  4. Submit within the deadline — Most payers require appeals within 30-180 days.
  5. Follow up regularly — Track your appeal status.

Essential Questions for Your Appeal:

  • "What specific documentation is needed to resolve this denial?"
  • "What is the appeal deadline for this denial?"
  • "Can this claim be corrected and resubmitted?"
  • "Is there a peer-to-peer review option available?"
