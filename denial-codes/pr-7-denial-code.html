PR-7

PR-7 Denial Code: Benefit Maximum Used Up

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Patient Responsibility

📋 What is it?

PR-7 is a Patient Responsibility code indicating the patient is responsible for costs because the benefit maximum for the specific service or overall plan has been reached.

🔍 Root Causes

  • Annual benefit limit reached
  • Lifetime benefit limit reached
  • Specific service limit (e.g., physical therapy visits) exceeded

✅ Resolution Process

1

Verify Benefit Limit

Verify the remaining benefit limit with the payer.

2

Inform the Patient

Inform the patient about the benefit exhaustion and their responsibility.

3

Offer Payment Options

Offer payment options for the remaining balance.

4

Investigate Appeals

Investigate if any policy exceptions or appeals are possible for continued coverage.

📞 What to Ask

Questions to ask internally or the payer to clarify the denial.

  • What is the annual/lifetime benefit limit for this service category or plan?
  • How much of the benefit limit has been used to date?
  • Are there any provisions for extending benefits or appealing the limit?

📝 Sample Letter

[Your Clinic/Hospital Letterhead]

[Date]

[Patient Name]
[Patient Address]

Subject: Important Information Regarding Your Recent Medical Services - Denial Code PR-7

Dear [Patient Name],

We are writing to inform you about a recent denial for services provided on [Date of Service]. The denial code associated with this claim is PR-7, which indicates that the benefit maximum for this service or your plan has been reached.

This means that you have utilized the full coverage amount allocated by your insurance plan for this type of service (or overall). Consequently, any further services of this nature will be your financial responsibility. The amount outstanding due to benefit exhaustion is [Specific Amount].

To discuss this matter and explore potential payment options, please contact our billing department at [Phone Number] or [Email Address]. We can also help you understand your remaining benefits for other services if applicable.

Sincerely,
[Your Clinic/Hospital Name]

💡 Prevention

  • Monitor patient benefit usage throughout the benefit period.
  • Promptly inform patients when they are approaching their benefit maximums.
  • Advise patients on alternative coverage options or financial arrangements when limits are reached.
  • Ensure accurate tracking of services against benefit limits in the billing system.
