OA-23

OA-23 Denial Code: Payment Adjusted Due to Interest Penalty Clause

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Other Adjustment

📋 What is OA-23 Denial Code?

OA-23 is an Other Adjustment (OA) code indicating that the payer applied an interest penalty adjustment because the claim was not processed or paid within the required timeframe, or the claim was submitted late. This adjustment reflects contractual or regulatory interest penalties owed to the provider.

OA-23 adjustments are typically triggered when payers fail to meet timely payment requirements or when state regulations mandate interest payments on late claims. This is distinct from claim denials and represents a financial penalty applied to the allowed amount.

🔍 Root Causes of OA-23 Adjustments

⏳

Payer Processing Delay

  • Payer failed to process claim within required timeframe
  • Internal payer processing backlog caused delays
  • Claim held up in payer review queues
  • Payer system errors delayed adjudication
  • Information requests from payer caused processing delays
💰

Interest Penalty Trigger

  • Late payment triggers contractual interest penalties
  • Statutory interest requirements activated
  • Payer contract specifies interest for delayed payments
  • Claim exceeded maximum processing timeframe
  • Payment issued after the required deadline
📜

State Mandate

  • Some states require interest on late insurance payments
  • State insurance regulations dictate penalty rates
  • State-mandated prompt payment laws triggered
  • Regulatory compliance requirements enforced
  • State penalty calculations applied to claim
📝

Contractual Obligation

  • Payer contract specifies interest penalties for delays
  • Network agreement includes prompt payment clauses
  • Group health plan contractual requirements
  • Self-funded plan administrator obligations
  • Contract terms exceed state minimum requirements

✅ Step-by-Step Resolution Process

1

Verify the Interest Penalty Calculation

Review the ERA/EOB to verify the interest penalty amount. Confirm the penalty was calculated correctly based on the applicable interest rate, the number of days delayed, and the allowed amount for the claim.

💡 Pro Tip: Interest penalties are typically calculated as a percentage of the allowed amount per day of delay. Verify the math matches the contract or state regulation.
2

Confirm Original Claim Submission Date

Verify the date the original claim was submitted to the payer. This establishes the baseline for calculating the processing timeframe and determines whether the payer exceeded the allowed number of days for adjudication.

3

Check State Regulations on Interest Penalties

Research your state's insurance regulations regarding interest penalties on late claim payments. Many states have prompt payment laws that mandate specific interest rates and timeframes for claim processing.

⚠️ Important: State prompt payment laws vary significantly. Some states have no mandatory interest provisions, while others require 10-18% annual interest on late payments.
4

Review Payer Contract Terms

Examine the provider's contract with the payer for prompt payment clauses and interest penalty provisions. Compare the contractual terms with the actual adjustment applied to ensure the payer applied the correct rate and calculation method.

5

File Appeal if Penalty is Incorrect

If the interest penalty calculation is incorrect or the payer has misapplied the penalty, file a formal appeal. Include documentation of the original submission date, applicable contract terms or state regulations, and a corrected calculation if applicable.

6

Document for Tracking and Reporting

Maintain records of all OA-23 adjustments for tracking and reporting purposes. Monitor payer payment patterns to identify systemic delays and use this data for contract negotiations or regulatory complaints if necessary.

📞 What to Ask the Payer Representative

Essential Questions for OA-23 Adjustments:

  1. "What triggered the interest penalty on this claim?"
  2. "What interest rate was applied, and what is the basis for this rate?"
  3. "What is the calculation period used for the interest penalty?"
  4. "Is this penalty applied per state regulation or per contractual obligation?"
  5. "What was the original claim submission date in your system?"
  6. "What was the adjudication date for this claim?"
  7. "Can you provide a detailed breakdown of the interest calculation?"
  8. "Is there a specific state statute or contract clause that governs this penalty?"
  9. "What is the process for disputing an incorrect interest penalty calculation?"
  10. "May I have a reference number for this conversation?"

💡 Prevention Strategies

Reduce OA-23 Adjustments with These Best Practices:

  • Track Claim Processing Timelines: Monitor how long each payer takes to process claims and flag delays early
  • Know State Prompt Pay Laws: Understand your state's timely filing and payment regulations
  • Review Payer Contracts: Ensure contracts include clear prompt payment clauses and interest penalty provisions
  • Submit Clean Claims: Reduce processing delays by submitting error-free claims with complete documentation
  • Follow Up Proactively: Contact payers when claims exceed normal processing timeframes
  • Maintain Submission Records: Keep proof of claim submission dates for all claims
  • Monitor ERA Timeliness: Track when ERAs are received relative to claim submission dates
  • Use Electronic Submission: Electronic claims have faster processing times than paper claims
  • Document All Communications: Keep records of all payer contacts regarding claim status
  • Report Systematic Delays: File complaints with state insurance departments for pattern violations
