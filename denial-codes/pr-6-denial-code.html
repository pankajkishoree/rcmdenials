PR-6

PR-6 Denial Code: Coinsurance Amount

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Patient Responsibility

📋 What is it?

PR-6 is a Patient Responsibility code indicating that the patient is responsible for a coinsurance amount, which is a percentage of the allowed costs for services.

🔍 Root Causes

  • Payer covered portion (patient pays % coinsurance)
  • Out-of-network coinsurance rates
  • Benefit maximum for the service category has been exceeded

✅ Resolution Process

1

Verify Coinsurance

Verify the coinsurance percentage with the payer.

2

Bill the Patient

Bill the patient for their coinsurance portion.

3

Explain Coinsurance

Explain the coinsurance concept and calculation to the patient.

4

Offer Payment Options

Offer payment options for the patient's share.

📞 What to Ask

Questions to ask internally or the payer to clarify the denial.

  • What is the patient's coinsurance percentage for this specific service?
  • What is the allowed amount for this service, and what is the patient's calculated coinsurance?
  • Does the coinsurance percentage change for out-of-network providers?

📝 Sample Letter

[Your Clinic/Hospital Letterhead]

[Date]

[Patient Name]
[Patient Address]

Subject: Important Information Regarding Your Recent Medical Services - Denial Code PR-6

Dear [Patient Name],

We are writing to inform you about a recent denial for services provided on [Date of Service]. The denial code associated with this claim is PR-6, which indicates that you are responsible for a coinsurance amount.

Coinsurance is your share of the costs of a covered healthcare service, calculated as a percentage (e.g., 20%) of the allowed amount for the service. For the services rendered, your coinsurance responsibility is [Specific Coinsurance Amount or Percentage].

To resolve this, please remit the amount of [Specific Coinsurance Amount] to our billing department. We offer payment plans to assist you. Please contact our billing department at [Phone Number] or [Email Address] to discuss your options.

Sincerely,
[Your Clinic/Hospital Name]

💡 Prevention

  • Obtain and verify patient's coinsurance details from their insurance provider.
  • Clearly communicate the patient's coinsurance responsibility and the calculation method.
  • Educate patients on how coinsurance applies to different services and network statuses.
  • Bill patients accurately for their defined coinsurance.
