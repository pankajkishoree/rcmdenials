CO-50

CO-50 Denial Code: These Are Non-Covered Services

Last Updated: June 2026 | Category: Contractual Obligation

📋 What is CO-50 Denial Code?

CO-50 indicates that the service provided is not covered under the patient's insurance plan. This means the service is excluded from the benefit package, either by policy design or because it doesn't meet medical necessity criteria.

This denial typically results in patient responsibility, but only if proper advance notice (ABN for Medicare) was provided.

🔍 Common Reasons for CO-50 Denials

📋

Plan Exclusions

  • Service explicitly excluded in policy
  • Cosmetic procedures
  • Experimental/investigational treatments
  • Over-the-counter items
  • Comfort items
  • Services not FDA-approved
⚕️

Medical Necessity

  • Service deemed not medically necessary
  • Insufficient documentation
  • Diagnosis doesn't support procedure
  • Alternative treatment available
  • Frequency limits exceeded
🚫

Benefit Limitations

  • Annual/lifetime limits reached
  • Service frequency exceeded
  • Age restrictions
  • Gender-specific exclusions
  • Preventive service limits
📝

Coverage Criteria

  • Prior authorization not obtained
  • Step therapy not followed
  • Out-of-network provider
  • Non-contracted facility
  • Coverage criteria not met

✅ How to Handle CO-50 Denials

1

Verify the Denial Reason

Contact the payer to understand the specific reason for the non-coverage determination.

Questions to Ask:
  • "Why specifically is this service not covered?"
  • "Is this a plan exclusion or a medical necessity denial?"
  • "What policy or guideline supports this denial?"
  • "Can you provide a copy of the coverage policy?"
  • "Are there any circumstances where this service would be covered?"
2

Review Medical Documentation

Examine the medical record to determine if the service was truly medically necessary and properly documented.

  • Check diagnosis codes support medical necessity
  • Verify documentation is complete
  • Review clinical notes for justification
  • Confirm correct procedure codes used
3

Determine if Appeal is Warranted

Decide whether to appeal based on the denial reason and available evidence.

Appeal if:
  • Service IS covered but payer applied wrong policy
  • Medical necessity is clearly documented
  • Diagnosis code was incorrect/incomplete
  • Payer has incorrect benefit information
  • Prior authorization was obtained
Don't Appeal if:
  • Service is explicitly excluded by plan
  • Cosmetic/elective procedure without coverage
  • Experimental treatment not covered
  • Benefit limit legitimately reached
4

Check for ABN/Waiver

For Medicare and some commercial plans, verify if an Advance Beneficiary Notice (ABN) was signed.

⚠️ Important: Without a valid ABN, you cannot bill the patient for Medicare non-covered services. The charge must be written off.
5

Bill Patient Appropriately

If service is legitimately non-covered and proper notice was given, transfer balance to patient.

  • Verify ABN or waiver on file
  • Provide clear explanation to patient
  • Include copy of EOB with statement
  • Offer payment plan options

📋 Advance Beneficiary Notice (ABN) Requirements

When to Use an ABN (Medicare):

  • You believe Medicare will deny the service as not medically necessary
  • Service exceeds frequency limits
  • Service is typically not covered
  • Patient requests non-covered service

ABN Must Include:

  • Specific service(s) that may be denied
  • Reason Medicare may not pay
  • Estimated cost to patient
  • Patient signature and date
⚠️ Critical: ABN must be signed BEFORE service is provided. Retroactive ABNs are not valid.

💡 Prevention Strategies

  • Pre-Service Verification: Verify coverage and medical necessity criteria before service
  • Obtain Prior Authorization: Get authorization for services requiring pre-approval
  • Use ABNs Appropriately: Have patients sign ABN when coverage is uncertain
  • Check Coverage Policies: Review payer medical policies before scheduling
  • Document Medical Necessity: Ensure thorough documentation of clinical rationale
  • Verify Benefits: Confirm specific service is covered under patient's plan
  • Patient Education: Inform patients of potential non-coverage before service
  • Alternative Codes: Consider if different code would be covered
  • Appeal Timely: File appeals quickly with strong clinical documentation

📊 Common Non-Covered Services

Cosmetic Procedures

Rhinoplasty, liposuction, breast augmentation (unless reconstructive)

Experimental Treatments

Investigational drugs, non-FDA approved devices

Comfort Items

Bed rails, air conditioners, humidifiers

Routine Foot Care

Nail trimming (unless diabetic with complications)

Vision Services

Routine eye exams, glasses (many plans)

Dental Services

Routine dental care under medical plans
