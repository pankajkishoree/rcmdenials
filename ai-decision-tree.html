AI-Powered Denial Resolution Decision Tree

Claim denial resolution requires scenario-specific response strategies — choosing the wrong path wastes time and delays recovery. RCM Denials' AI Decision Tree Engine guides revenue cycle teams through resolution workflows using interactive branching logic tailored to each denial type, payer, and clinical scenario.

Branching Logic Architecture

Select the denial scenario matching your situation. The tool presents sequential branching questions based on your responses, with each branch leading to specific next steps, required documentation, and recommended actions. This targeted approach reduces resolution time by 40-60% compared to manual research and generic resolution templates.

Denial Categories Supported

  • Eligibility denials — CO-4, CO-18, CO-56 and related eligibility verification failures
  • Coding denials — CO-11, CO-16, CO-22 and coding-related rejection scenarios
  • Authorization denials — Missing, invalid, or expired prior authorization issues
  • Coordination of benefits — CO-23, CO-29 and primary/secondary payer sequencing disputes
  • Timely filing — CO-29, PR-204 and filing deadline complication pathways
  • Medical necessity — Clinical justification challenges and peer-to-peer review triggers

Evidence-Based Resolution Paths

Every branch and recommendation is derived from real-world denial management data. The scenarios are built from thousands of actual denial cases, and resolution paths reflect empirically validated approaches — not just CMS guideline interpretations. Each step includes timeline expectations, escalation criteria, and documentation requirements.

Payer-Specific Adaptations

The decision tree accounts for payer-specific appeal processes, documentation requirements, and escalation pathways. Medicare, Medicaid, and commercial payer denials follow distinct resolution workflows, and the tool routes users through the correct process for their specific payer context.

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of denials does the decision tree cover?
The tool covers all major denial categories including eligibility, coding, authorization, coordination of benefits, timely filing, and medical necessity denials. Each category has multiple sub-scenarios based on specific denial codes and payer responses.
How does the branching logic work?
After selecting your denial scenario, the tool asks a series of questions about your specific situation. Your answers determine which branch of the decision tree you follow, leading to resolution steps tailored to your exact denial type and circumstances.
Can I use this for Medicare and commercial payer denials?
Yes. The decision tree covers denials from Medicare, Medicaid, and all major commercial payers. The resolution paths account for payer-specific requirements and appeal processes.
What information does the tool provide at each step?
Each branch provides specific next steps, required documentation, recommended actions, timeline expectations, and escalation criteria. You'll know exactly what to do, what to include, and when to escalate to the next level.
Is this better than calling the payer directly?
The decision tree helps you prepare before calling the payer. By understanding your denial type and gathering the right documentation upfront, your payer calls are more productive and resolution happens faster. The tool also provides alternative resolution paths that don't require payer contact.
