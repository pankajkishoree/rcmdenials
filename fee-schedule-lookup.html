How the Medicare Physician Fee Schedule Works

The Medicare Physician Fee Schedule (MPFS) is the system CMS uses to determine how much it pays physicians and other qualified healthcare professionals for over 10,000 services and procedures. Updated annually, the fee schedule is based on a Resource-Based Relative Value System (RBRVS) that assigns Relative Value Units (RVUs) to each CPT code, then multiplies the total by a national conversion factor.

The Medicare Payment Formula

Every Medicare payment for a physician service follows this formula:

Adjusted RVUs = (Work RVU × Work GPCI) + (PE RVU × PE GPCI) + (MP RVU × MP GPCI)
Medicare Payment = Adjusted Total RVUs × Conversion Factor ($32.7442 for CY 2024)

What Are the Three RVU Components?

  • Work RVU (wRVU) — Reflects the physician's time, skill, training, and intensity involved in delivering the service. This is the component that represents physician effort and is used for productivity tracking.
  • Practice Expense RVU (PE RVU) — Covers the overhead costs of running a practice — staff salaries, office rent, medical supplies, and equipment. PE RVUs differ between facility and non-facility settings.
  • Malpractice RVU (MP RVU) — Represents the cost of professional liability (malpractice) insurance. It is the smallest component and varies by specialty risk profile.

What Is the GPCI and Why Does It Matter?

Geographic Practice Cost Indices (GPCIs) are locality-specific multipliers applied to each RVU component to reflect local cost differences. CMS updates GPCIs every few years based on local wage surveys, office rental costs, and malpractice insurance premiums. High-cost metropolitan areas like New York City, San Francisco, and Boston have GPCIs above 1.0, which increase physician payments. Rural areas often have lower GPCIs. Note: Federal law requires the Work GPCI to be floored at a minimum of 1.0 — no locality can receive less than the national average for physician work.

Facility vs. Non-Facility Rates

Medicare pays two different PE RVU rates based on the setting where a service is provided:

  • Non-Facility (Office, POS 11) — Higher PE RVU because the physician's practice bears the full overhead cost. This applies when services are performed in the physician's own office or outpatient clinic.
  • Facility (Hospital, POS 21; Outpatient Hospital, POS 22; ASC, POS 24) — Lower PE RVU because CMS separately pays the hospital or ASC for overhead costs through the OPPS or ASC fee schedules. Physicians in facility settings receive only the professional component of PE RVUs.

Understanding the Medicare Conversion Factor

The conversion factor (CF) is a dollar amount that converts adjusted RVUs into a payment. Congress sets budget neutrality requirements that affect the CF annually. The CY 2024 CF is $32.7442, down from $33.8872 in CY 2023 due to budget neutrality adjustments. For 2025 modeling, our tool allows you to manually override the CF to reflect the projected CY 2025 value.

Frequently Asked Questions

Medicare physician fee schedule payments are calculated using the formula: Payment = [(Work RVU × Work GPCI) + (PE RVU × PE GPCI) + (MP RVU × MP GPCI)] × Conversion Factor. For CY 2024, the national conversion factor is $32.7442. RVUs are set annually by CMS based on the AMA RUC recommendations and adjusted by GPCI values for geographic cost differences.
Work RVU (wRVU) measures physician time, complexity, and effort. Practice Expense RVU (PE RVU) covers practice overhead — staff, supplies, equipment — and is higher in non-facility settings where the practice bears these costs directly. Malpractice RVU (MP RVU) represents the professional liability insurance premium associated with the service. All three are summed (after GPCI adjustments) and multiplied by the conversion factor to produce the fee schedule payment.
GPCI (Geographic Practice Cost Index) is a locality multiplier CMS applies to each RVU component to account for regional cost differences. Physicians in high-cost areas like San Francisco (PE GPCI ~1.272) earn more than national average. Physicians in rural areas may have GPCIs below 1.0. The Work GPCI has a statutory floor of 1.0. GPCIs are updated every 3 years using wage surveys, rental costs, and malpractice insurance data.
Commercial estimates are modeled from publicly available industry benchmarks and represent typical reimbursement ranges as a percentage of Medicare. They are not actual contracted rates. Actual commercial rates are proprietary, negotiated individually, and vary significantly by specialty, provider group size, geography, and contract vintage. Use these estimates for planning, benchmarking, and contract negotiation preparation only — not for billing.
RVU values are sourced from the CMS Medicare Physician Fee Schedule Public Use Files (PUF) for Calendar Year 2024, available at cms.gov. The conversion factor of $32.7442 is the CY 2024 Medicare conversion factor published in the CY 2024 Medicare Physician Fee Schedule Final Rule. GPCI values are from the CMS 2024 GPCI schedule published in the Federal Register.
