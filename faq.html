General
What is RCM Denials?

RCM Denials is a free, AI-powered medical billing and claim denial management platform. It provides tools for claim denial prediction, CPT & ICD-10 coding validation, modifier checking, timely filing calculation, payer directory lookup, medical necessity checks, and claims tracking — all running directly in your browser.

Is RCM Denials really free?

Yes, RCM Denials is 100% free with no hidden fees, subscriptions, or premium tiers. All tools including the AI Claim Denial Predictor, Coding Checker, Modifier Validator, Timely Filing Calculator, Payer Directory, Claims Tracker, Necessity Checklist, and Call Notes Generator are available at no cost.

Do I need to create an account?

No. RCM Denials requires no registration, login, or account creation. Simply visit the website and start using the tools immediately.

What makes RCM Denials different from other billing tools?

RCM Denials is completely free, requires no account, stores all data locally for privacy, and provides AI-powered assistance specifically designed for AR callers. Most billing software costs thousands per year and requires patient data — RCM Denials does neither.

Can I use RCM Denials on my phone?

Yes. RCM Denials is a responsive web application that works on desktops, tablets, and mobile phones. You can use it directly in your browser without installing any software.

What browsers are supported?

RCM Denials works on all modern browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge, and their mobile versions. We recommend using the latest browser version for the best experience.

AI Tools & Features
How does the AI Claim Denial Predictor work?

The AI Claim Denial Predictor analyzes your claim data before submission to identify potential denial risks. It checks for coding errors, missing modifiers, compliance issues, and payer-specific requirements, giving you a risk score and actionable recommendations to prevent denials.

How does the CPT & ICD-10 Coding Checker help?

The Coding Checker validates CPT and ICD-10 code combinations for medical necessity, checks for bundling conflicts, verifies payer compatibility, and identifies common coding errors that lead to claim denials.

What is the CPT Modifier Validator?

The Modifier Validator checks your CPT modifier usage for accuracy, identifies modifier conflicts (like modifier 25 and 59 together), verifies proper bundling compliance, and ensures modifiers meet payer-specific requirements.

How does the Timely Filing Calculator work?

The Timely Filing Calculator computes exact filing deadlines based on the payer, claim type, date of service, and denial date. It accounts for payer-specific timely filing rules and alerts you before deadlines expire to prevent permanent claim loss.

What is the Claim Denial Resolution Assistant?

The Resolution Assistant provides guided next steps based on denial codes, payer rules, and what the payer representative says during a call. It helps you navigate complex denial scenarios with intelligent recommendations for escalation, resubmission, or appeal.

Does the Medical Claim Denial Checklist customize per payer?

Yes. The Denial Checklist generates payer-specific and denial-specific question lists tailored to the insurance company you're calling. This ensures you ask the right questions for every call scenario.

How does the AI Medical Call Notes Generator work?

After a payer call, the AI Call Notes Generator creates professional AR summary notes with one click. It includes session details, denial information, call documentation, follow-up actions, and next steps — ready to paste into your billing software.

What is the Medical Claims Tracker?

The Claims Tracker lets you manage multiple claims in parallel with status updates, follow-up reminders, recovery analytics, and priority sorting. It helps AR teams stay organized and never miss a follow-up.

Can I export data from RCM Denials?

Yes. You can export session data, call notes, and claims as CSV files. The AI Call Notes Generator also creates formatted text ready to paste into billing systems.

Is there a limit on how many claims I can check?

No. There are no usage limits. You can check as many claims, validate as many codes, and generate as many notes as you need without any restrictions.

Denial Codes & Billing
What denial codes does RCM Denials cover?

RCM Denials covers 600+ denial codes including CO (Contractual Obligation), PR (Patient Responsibility), OA (Other Adjustment), and PI (Payer Initiated) codes. Each code includes causes, resolution steps, appeal templates, and prevention strategies.

Can I use RCM Denials for specialty-specific billing?

Yes. RCM Denials includes specialty-specific CPT code guides for cardiology, dermatology, emergency medicine, family medicine, gastroenterology, and many more specialties. Each guide includes top codes, billing tips, and denial prevention strategies.

How does the ICD-10 Code Lookup work?

The ICD-10 Code Lookup lets you search the complete ICD-10-CM code set by code number, description, or keyword. Each code includes clinical context, coding guidelines, and links to related denial codes and billing resources.

Which insurance payers are in the directory?

The Insurance Payer Directory covers major commercial payers (UnitedHealthcare, Aetna, Cigna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Humana, Kaiser), government programs (Medicare, Medicaid, Tricare, VA), and managed care organizations. Each entry includes direct phone numbers, IVR shortcuts, and department details.

How accurate are the AI-generated suggestions?

Our AI tools provide strong guidance based on established billing rules, payer guidelines, and denial code databases. However, all suggestions should be verified with the respective payer before taking action. Medical billing requirements vary by payer and are subject to change.

How often is the denial code database updated?

We regularly update the denial code database, payer directory, and tool logic to reflect current billing standards, new payer rules, and updated denial codes. Updates are applied automatically — no action needed from users.

Privacy & Security
Does RCM Denials store my patient data?

No. All data you enter (claim numbers, denial codes, payer names, notes) is stored locally in your browser using localStorage and IndexedDB. Nothing is transmitted to external servers. We recommend not entering direct patient identifiers like names or Social Security numbers.

Is this a HIPAA-compliant tool?

RCM Denials is designed with privacy-first architecture — all data stays in your browser. We recommend not entering PHI (patient names, SSNs) into the tool. Use claim numbers and billing identifiers only. For full HIPAA compliance, consult your organization's compliance officer.

Is my data safe when I use RCM Denials?

Yes. Your data never leaves your browser. RCM Denials uses localStorage and IndexedDB for all data storage — nothing is sent to external servers. You can clear all data at any time through your browser settings or the platform's built-in reset function.
