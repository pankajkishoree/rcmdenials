Privacy Policy

How we protect your data and privacy

Home / Privacy Policy

Effective Date: June 25, 2026 · Last Updated: June 28, 2026

1. Introduction

Welcome to RCM Denials ("we," "our," or "us"). We are committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our AI-powered medical billing and claim denial management platform at rcmdenials.com.

By using RCM Denials, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the platform.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Data Stored Locally in Your Browser

RCM Denials is designed with a privacy-first architecture. The following data is stored entirely within your browser using localStorage and IndexedDB — it is never transmitted to our servers:

  • Claim numbers, denial codes, and payer names you enter
  • Call session notes and AR documentation
  • Timely filing calculations and history
  • Batch claims data and tracker entries
  • Theme preferences and UI settings

2.2 Anonymous Usage Data

We may collect anonymous, non-identifying analytics data to improve the platform, including:

  • Pages visited and features used
  • Session duration and frequency
  • Browser type and device information
  • Screen resolution and viewport size

This data contains no patient information, claim details, or personal identifiers.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the limited data we collect to:

  • Provide and maintain the RCM Denials platform
  • Improve user experience and platform functionality
  • Analyze usage trends and optimize performance
  • Respond to user inquiries and provide support
  • Detect and prevent technical issues

4. Data Storage & Security

All patient and claim-related data you enter into RCM Denials is stored locally in your browser using localStorage and IndexedDB. This data never leaves your device. We do not operate databases that store your Protected Health Information (PHI).

We implement reasonable security measures to protect any anonymous usage data we do collect, including HTTPS encryption, Content Security Policy headers, and access controls.

5. HIPAA Considerations

Important: RCM Denials is designed with privacy in mind. We strongly recommend that you do not enter real patient names, Social Security numbers, or other direct identifiers into the application. The tool is designed to work with claim numbers, denial codes, and billing identifiers — not full patient records.

For full HIPAA compliance, consult your organization's compliance officer. RCM Denials is not a covered entity under HIPAA.

6. Cookies & Local Storage

We use cookies, localStorage, and IndexedDB for platform functionality. For full details, see our Cookie Policy.

  • Essential cookies — Required for platform functionality (theme preferences, session state)
  • localStorage — Saves UI preferences and settings
  • IndexedDB — Stores call sessions, claims data, and analytics locally

7. Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services:

  • Google Analytics — For anonymous usage analytics. Google Analytics uses cookies to collect anonymous traffic data. Google's Privacy Policy
  • Cloudflare — For CDN hosting, security, and performance optimization. Cloudflare's Privacy Policy
  • Google Fonts — For typography. Google Fonts may collect anonymous request data.

These services do not receive any patient data, claim information, or personal identifiers from RCM Denials.

8. Data Sharing

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties. We may share anonymous, aggregated usage data with service providers who assist in operating our platform, subject to confidentiality obligations.

9. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Clear all locally stored data at any time via your browser settings
  • Export your session data as CSV from within the platform
  • Opt out of anonymous analytics tracking by disabling cookies
  • Request information about any data we may hold about you
  • Request deletion of any anonymous usage data we have collected

10. GDPR Compliance

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have additional rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • Right to access your personal data
  • Right to rectification of inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object to processing

Since RCM Denials stores all data locally in your browser and collects only anonymous usage data, most GDPR rights are exercised directly through your browser and device.

11. CCPA Compliance

If you are a California resident, you have rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

  • Right to know what personal information is collected
  • Right to delete personal information
  • Right to opt out of the sale of personal information
  • Right to non-discrimination for exercising your rights

RCM Denials does not sell personal information. All claim data is stored locally and is under your control.

12. Data Retention

Your locally stored data (claim sessions, notes, tracker entries) remains in your browser until you choose to delete it. Anonymous usage analytics may be retained for up to 26 months for trend analysis.

13. Children's Privacy

RCM Denials is not intended for use by children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

14. Changes to This Policy

We reserve the right to update this Privacy Policy at any time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated effective date. Your continued use of RCM Denials after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

15. Contact Us
