Pre-Submission Claim Risk Analysis for Healthcare Organizations

Claim denials cost U.S. healthcare organizations over $262 billion annually, with the average denied claim requiring $118 in rework to resolve. RCM Denials' Claim Risk Review tool analyzes claims before submission, identifying denial risks across six critical dimensions — patient eligibility, insurance requirements, provider credentials, medical necessity, documentation completeness, and prior authorization status.

Six-Dimension Risk Scoring

Each claim is evaluated against a comprehensive rule engine built on CMS guidelines, NCCI edits, and payer-specific policies. The tool assigns individual scores across all six dimensions, then calculates an overall risk score from 0 to 100. Claims scoring above 70 flag high-probability denial scenarios that require immediate attention before submission.

Denial Categories Detected

  • Medical necessity mismatches — ICD-10 codes insufficient to support billed CPT procedures
  • Modifier omissions — Required modifiers missing for specific payer contracts or procedure combinations
  • Eligibility verification failures — Patient insurance status issues triggering automatic adjudication denials
  • Coordination of benefits errors — Incorrect primary and secondary payer sequencing
  • Prior authorization gaps — Procedures requiring pre-approval that were not obtained or expired
  • Billing frequency violations — Codes exceeding allowed frequency thresholds within defined timeframes

Enterprise Deployment

The tool supports both individual claim reviews and batch processing workflows. Revenue cycle teams use it as a final quality gate before electronic submission to clearinghouses. Input complete claim data across all six steps for maximum assessment accuracy — the more detail provided, including modifiers, place of service, and clinical notes, the more precise the risk analysis.

Frequently Asked Questions

Our rule engine uses CMS guidelines, CCI edits, and payer-specific rules to identify denial risks with high accuracy. The tool catches the most common denial reasons that account for over 80% of all claim denials in the healthcare industry.

Yes. The tool analyzes claim elements against universal coding rules and medical necessity guidelines. It works for Medicare, Medicaid, and all major commercial payers including UnitedHealthcare, Aetna, Cigna, and Blue Cross Blue Shield.

Absolutely. The tool is designed for both individual claim reviews and batch processing workflows. Many billing teams use it as a final quality check before electronic claim submission to clearhouses.

The results page provides specific, actionable steps to fix each identified risk factor. You'll see recommended review actions, potential denial categories, and a confidence level indicating how certain the assessment is for each finding.
