Payer-Specific Timely Filing Deadline Calculator

Timely filing denials are among the most preventable — and most costly — denial categories in healthcare billing. When a claim misses the payer's filing deadline, there is no appeal pathway and no revenue recovery. RCM Denials' Insurance Timely Filing Calculator computes exact filing deadlines for 48+ insurance payers, providing color-coded status indicators for immediate assessment.

Deadline Calculation Methodology

Select your payer, claim type, and date of service. The calculator generates multiple deadline scenarios: original filing deadline, timely filing limit from denial date, and appeal filing windows. Each deadline is color-coded — green for safe, amber for approaching, red for expired — enabling instant prioritization of filing urgency.

Payer-Specific Variations

Timely filing limits are not standardized across payers. Medicare allows 12 months from date of service for original claims. Commercial payers set their own limits — some as short as 90 days. Medicaid timelines vary by state. The calculator accounts for these contractual variations and applies the correct deadline for each specific payer.

Critical Filing Windows Tracked

  • Original claim filing — Initial deadline for clean claim submission
  • Appeal filing deadlines — Compressed windows for denied claim appeals
  • Medicare redetermination — First-level appeal timelines specific to CMS
  • QIC and ALJ windows — Second and third-level appeal filing requirements
  • Payer contractual overrides — Contractual variations superseding standard timelines

Operational Use Cases

AR teams, billing managers, and denial management specialists use this calculator to prioritize claim submission queues, identify at-risk claims approaching deadline thresholds, and build filing calendars across multi-payer portfolios. The tool is essential for practices managing claims across payers with differing filing requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the timely filing deadline for Medicare claims?
Medicare allows 12 months from the date of service for original claims, and 120 days from the date of the denial notice for appeals. However, Medicare Advantage plans may have different timelines. Our calculator provides the exact deadline for your specific claim scenario.
How do timely filing limits differ between payers?
Each payer sets its own timely filing limits. Commercial payers typically range from 90 days to 1 year from date of service. Medicaid varies by state. The tool covers 48+ payers and automatically applies the correct deadline based on your selection.
Can I get an extension on a timely filing deadline?
Some payers allow extensions for good cause exceptions, such as demonstrated system failures or natural disasters. However, these exceptions are rare and require documentation. The tool flags when you're approaching a deadline so you can submit before extensions become necessary.
What happens if I miss a timely filing deadline?
Claims filed after the timely filing limit are automatically denied with a CO-29 (timely filing) or PR-204 (timely filing) denial code. Unlike most denials, timely filing denials cannot be appealed unless you can prove the claim was filed within the deadline. Prevention is the best strategy.
Does the calculator include appeal deadlines?
Yes. The tool calculates both original filing deadlines and appeal filing windows. For denied claims, you'll see the deadline for redetermination requests, second-level appeals, and any other applicable appeal stages based on the denial date.
