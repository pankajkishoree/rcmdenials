📋 Documentation Requirements

  • ? Oncologist order with specific drug name, dose, route, and cycle number documented
  • ? Chemotherapy consent form signed and dated prior to administration
  • ? Pre-chemotherapy labs (CBC, CMP, tumor markers) documented with results
  • ? Infusion start and end times documented for accurate time-based billing
  • ? Drug administration details including lot number, expiration date, and manufacturer
  • ? Pre-medication orders and administration documented (antiemetics, steroids, hydration)
  • ? Vital signs monitored during infusion with any adverse reactions documented
  • ? Treatment protocol reference (e.g., FOLFOX, R-CHOP, AC-T) documented in chart
  • ? Response to treatment documented including imaging and lab results
  • ? Tumor staging and histopathology documented for initial diagnosis claims

⏱️ Global Surgery Period

Global Period Rules:

0 days for chemotherapy administration. 0 days for radiation therapy treatments. Surgical oncology procedures follow standard global surgery periods (10-90 days depending on procedure).

✅ Medical Necessity Tips

  • ? Document specific cancer diagnosis with ICD-10 code, stage, and histopathology
  • ? Include prior treatment history and response to demonstrate ongoing need for therapy
  • ? Reference NCCN guidelines or institutional protocols for treatment regimen justification
  • ? Document performance status (ECOG/Karnofsky) to support treatment appropriateness
  • ? Include molecular/biomarker testing results when using targeted therapy agents
  • ? Document reason for treatment regimen change with clinical rationale
  • ? For palliative care, document symptom burden and quality-of-life impact
  • ? Include lab values (tumor markers, CBC, CMP) supporting treatment response assessment

💰 Billing Guidelines

  • ? Bill chemotherapy base code based on actual infusion time  not scheduled time
  • ? Use sequential billing for multiple agents  base code for first hour, add-on for each additional
  • ? Bill blood product administration separately from chemotherapy when applicable
  • ? Ensure correct place of service  infusion center (49) vs office (11) vs hospital outpatient (22)
  • ? For observation stays, use correct discharge codes (99217-99220) based on documentation
  • ? Bill radiation therapy with treatment area documentation and field count
  • ? Monitor NCCI edits for chemo administration bundling with supportive services
  • ? For telehealth oncology visits, document clinical rationale for remote consultation

📝 Example Claim Scenarios

IV Chemotherapy Administration (FOLFOX)

CPT + ICD-10: 96413 (Chemo IV 1st hour) + 96415 (add-on 2nd hour) + 96417 (5-FU subq) + C18.9 (Colon cancer)

Modifiers: -25 (E/M if separate visit), -59 (distinct services)

Notes: 62-year-old with Stage III colon cancer receiving FOLFOX6 cycle 8. Oxaliplatin infusion 2 hours, leucovorin 30 min, 5-FU bolus and 46-hour infusion via pump. Documentation includes pre-chemo labs, consent, infusion times, and vital signs. E/M separately billed for treatment plan review.

Radiation Therapy Prostate Cancer

CPT + ICD-10: 77427 (Whole pelvis radiation 5 fields) + C61 (Prostate cancer)

Modifiers: -TC/-26 (split billing in hospital outpatient)

Notes: 68-year-old with localized prostate cancer receiving IMRT. Treatment plan documented with field arrangement and dose distribution. Simulation performed separately. Hospital bills technical component, radiation oncologist bills professional component with modifier 26.

Office Visit with Chemo Order

CPT + ICD-10: 99214 (E/M established patient) + 96413 (Chemo IV 1st hour) + Z51.11 (Chemo encounter) + C50.919 (Breast cancer)

Modifiers: -25 (Separately identifiable E/M)

Notes: 55-year-old with metastatic breast cancer. E/M visit documents treatment response assessment and decision to continue current regimen. Same-day chemo administration with paclitaxel infusion. Distinct documentation for each service with separate time calculations.
