?? Documentation Requirements

  • ? Physician order with specific spinal level(s) and injection type documented
  • ? Pre-procedure imaging review (MRI, CT, or X-ray) showing pathology at targeted level
  • ? Informed consent signed and dated prior to procedure
  • ? Fluoroscopic or CT guidance images confirming needle placement at correct level
  • ? Documentation of contrast spread pattern confirming epidural or joint space placement
  • ? Specific medications, concentrations, and volumes injected documented
  • ? Pre-procedure and post-procedure pain scores (VAS or NRS scale)
  • ? Documentation of medical necessity including conservative treatment failure
  • ? Procedure note signed by the performing physician with time documented
  • ? Post-procedure vitals and neurological assessment documented

?? Global Surgery Period

Global Period Rules:

0 days for diagnostic nerve blocks and facet joint injections. 0 days for epidural steroid injections. 0 days for acupuncture procedures. 90 days for neurostimulator implantation. 90 days for intrathecal pump placement. Minor procedures like trigger point injections have 0-day global period.

?? Medical Necessity Tips

  • ? Always document the specific spinal level(s) targeted and clinical rationale for each level
  • ? Include conservative treatment history — physical therapy, medications, and duration of treatment
  • ? Document pre-procedure pain scores and functional limitations before each injection
  • ? Reference interventional pain management society guidelines for appropriate use criteria
  • ? Document post-procedure pain relief percentage to establish treatment efficacy for repeat injections
  • ? Ensure HPI includes onset, location, duration, character, aggravating/relieving factors for pain
  • ? For acupuncture, document specific diagnosis codes that qualify for coverage (e.g., chronic low back pain)
  • ? Document imaging findings that correlate with the clinical presentation at each targeted level

?? Billing Guidelines

  • ? Use base + add-on code structure for multi-level injections (64483+64484 for each additional level)
  • ? Bill imaging guidance separately when not bundled — check NCCI edits for each code pair
  • ? Ensure fluoroscopic or CT guidance is documented for codes requiring imaging guidance
  • ? Use modifier 25 for E/M visit on same day as injection if separately identifiable documentation exists
  • ? Bill acupuncture with correct time-based codes — 97810 for first 15 minutes, 97811 for each additional
  • ? Verify whether facility or professional component is being billed — split billing with modifier 26/TC
  • ? For radiofrequency ablation, ensure diagnostic facet block documentation is in chart prior to billing
  • ? Monitor LCD coverage policies — some payers limit injection frequency per rolling 12-month period

?? Example Claim Scenarios

Lumbar Transforaminal Epidural Injection

CPT + ICD-10: 64483 (Transforaminal epidural L4-L5) + 64484 (additional level L5-S1) + M54.4 (Lumbago with sciatica)

Modifiers: -LT (left side), -25 (separate E/M if applicable)

Notes: Patient presents with left-sided radicular pain. MRI confirms L4-L5 and L5-S1 disc herniation. Fluoroscopic guidance used. Documentation includes contrast spread pattern and post-procedure pain relief assessment.

Acupuncture for Chronic Low Back Pain

CPT + ICD-10: 97810 (Acupuncture initial 15 min) + 97811 (each additional 15 min x2) + M54.5 (Low back pain)

Modifiers: None required

Notes: Medicare patient with chronic low back pain. 8 needles placed without electrical stimulation. Treatment plan documented with frequency and expected duration. Time-based billing for total 45-minute session.

Facet Joint Injection with Neurolysis

CPT + ICD-10: 64493 (Facet joint injection lumbar single level) + 64495 (additional level) + M47.816 (Spondylosis lumbar)

Modifiers: -59 (distinct service for multi-level)

Notes: Patient with documented facet arthropathy on MRI. Two positive diagnostic facet blocks prior to therapeutic injection. Documentation includes pre/post VAS scores and fluoroscopic confirmation of needle placement.
