Pulmonology billing encompasses spirometry, pulmonary function testing, bronchoscopy, oxygen therapy, and respiratory services. 📋 Documentation Requirements

  • ? Physician order with clinical indication for pulmonary function testing
  • ? Complete clinical history including smoking status and occupational exposures
  • ? Spirometry results with interpretation (FEV1, FVC, FEV1/FVC ratio)
  • ? Bronchoscopy report with findings, biopsies obtained, and pathology results
  • ? Pulmonary function test reports including plethysmography and diffusing capacity
  • ? Imaging results (chest X-ray, CT scan) supporting clinical indication
  • ? Clinical history including symptom assessment, onset, duration, and severity
  • ? Prior treatment response documentation (medications, oxygen therapy)
  • ? Physician attestation of medical necessity for each procedure
  • ? Documentation of functional status and impact on daily activities

⏱️ Global Surgery Period

Global Period Rules:

0 days for most diagnostic procedures including spirometry, pulse oximetry, and ABG sampling. 0 days for diagnostic bronchoscopy (31622). 30-90 days for surgical procedures including therapeutic bronchoscopy, lung volume reduction surgery, and thoracotomy procedures. Check specific CPT code for exact global period.

✅ Medical Necessity Tips

  • ? Always document specific symptoms (dyspnea, cough, wheezing, hemoptysis) in clinical notes
  • ? Include smoking history and occupational exposure documentation for all PFT orders
  • ? Document prior conservative treatment failure before ordering bronchoscopy
  • ? Reference clinical guidelines (ATS/ERS) for appropriate use criteria for PFTs
  • ? Include FEV1/FVC ratio and percent predicted values in spirometry documentation
  • ? Ensure the HPI includes onset, location, duration, character, aggravating/relieving factors
  • ? Document physical examination findings (wheezing, diminished breath sounds, crackles)
  • ? For bronchoscopy, document specific lesion location, size, and clinical suspicion

💰 Billing Guidelines

  • ? Bill spirometry with modifier 26 for professional component and TC for technical component when split
  • ? Use modifier 59 for separately identifiable bronchoscopy procedures performed same day as E/M
  • ? Ensure correct ICD-10 specificity  J44.1 for COPD with exacerbation, J44.0 for COPD with infection
  • ? Monitor NCCI edits for bronchoscopy bundling  31622 vs 31623/31625/31631
  • ? For bronchoscopy, use add-on codes (31624, 31626, 31632) for additional lobes only
  • ? Bill E/M with modifier 25 when separately evaluating patient on same day as PFT or bronchoscopy
  • ? Use appropriate place of service  outpatient facility vs office-based pulmonary lab
  • ? Verify which CPT codes require physician supervision for technical component

📝 Example Claim Scenarios

COPD Exacerbation Workup

CPT + ICD-10: 94010 (Spirometry) + 94012 (with bronchodilator) + J44.1 (COPD with exacerbation)

Modifiers: -26 (Professional component if reading only)

Notes: Patient presents with acute worsening of dyspnea and sputum production. Spirometry shows FEV1/FVC ratio 0.45, FEV1 35% predicted. Post-bronchodilator improvement 8%. Documentation includes symptoms, smoking history, and prior PFT results.

Asthma Management with Spirometry

CPT + ICD-10: 94010 (Spirometry) + 94060 (methacholine challenge) + J45.30 (Mild persistent asthma)

Modifiers: -25 for E/M visit on same day

Notes: Patient with suspected asthma undergoes spirometry and methacholine challenge. Positive challenge confirms bronchial hyperreactivity. Documentation includes asthma symptoms, triggers, and medication history.

Lung Nodule Bronchoscopy

CPT + ICD-10: 31622 (Bronchoscopy) + 31623 (Brush biopsy) + J98.4 (Lung collapse) + D46.9 (Myelodysplastic syndrome)

Modifiers: -26/-TC for split billing in hospital outpatient setting

Notes: Patient with known myelodysplastic syndrome and new lung nodule on CT. Bronchoscopy with brush biopsy performed. Hospital bills technical component, pulmonologist bills professional component. Pathology pending.
