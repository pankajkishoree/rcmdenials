📄 Documentation Requirements

  • ✓ Physician signed plan of care with diagnosis, frequency, and duration of therapy
  • ✓ Initial evaluation documenting baseline functional status with objective measurements
  • ✓ Treatment notes for each session documenting exercises performed and patient response
  • ✓ Progress notes with measurable goals and functional outcomes every 10 visits or 30 days
  • ✓ Documentation of medical necessity including specific functional limitations treated
  • ✓ Re-evaluation documentation showing progress toward goals or plan modification
  • ✓ Home exercise program documented and provided to patient each visit
  • ✓ Co-treatment documentation when two therapists work with patient simultaneously
  • ✓ Time-based coding: document total skilled time and time per service code
  • ✓ Physician certification/re-certification of plan of care at required intervals

⏱️ Global Surgery Period

Global Period Rules:

Physical therapy services generally have no global surgery period. However, post-surgical PT is subject to the global period of the originating surgical procedure. PT provided during the global period of a surgical procedure requires modifier 79 if unrelated to the surgery. Post-op PT for joint replacement (CPT 27447) is covered under the surgical global period with appropriate documentation.

💡 Medical Necessity Tips

  • ✓ Document specific functional limitations that affect activities of daily living
  • ✓ Include objective baseline measurements (ROM, strength, balance) in initial evaluation
  • ✓ Set measurable, time-bound goals aligned with functional improvement (not just pain reduction)
  • ✓ Document skilled therapy services provided — what the therapist did that required licensure
  • ✓ Show progression of exercises and activities as patient improves — avoid repeating same plan
  • ✓ Document home exercise program and patient compliance with independent exercises
  • ✓ For Medicare, distinguish between skilled maintenance therapy and custodial maintenance
  • ✓ Include physician referral with specific diagnosis and treatment goals in documentation

📝 Billing Guidelines

  • ✓ Use time-based coding: each unit = 8 minutes of direct skilled therapy (midpoint rule)
  • ✓ Bill correct evaluation code based on complexity — 97161 (low), 97162 (moderate), 97163 (high)
  • ✓ Include GP modifier on all physical therapy claims for Medicare and most commercial payers
  • ✓ Bill modalities separately only when not bundled — check NCCI edits for each combination
  • ✓ For co-treatment, document skilled services provided by each therapist and bill appropriate units
  • ✓ Monitor therapy cap thresholds — use KX modifier when exceptions apply with documentation
  • ✓ Ensure physician plan of care is signed and dated within required timeframe
  • ✓ Bill re-evaluation (97164) when there is a significant change in patient condition or plan

📊 Example Claim Scenarios

Post-Total Knee Replacement Rehabilitation

CPT + ICD-10: 97162 (Evaluation moderate) + 97110 (Therapeutic exercises 3 units) + 97140 (Manual therapy 2 units) + Z96.651 (Right knee replacement)

Modifiers: -GP (Physical therapy plan of care)

Notes: Patient 2 weeks post right TKA. Evaluation documents limited ROM (0-70 degrees) and gait deviations. Treatment includes therapeutic exercises for strengthening and manual therapy for ROM. Goals: achieve 0-120 degrees ROM in 6 weeks.

Lumbar Radiculopathy Treatment

CPT + ICD-10: 97163 (Evaluation high complexity) + 97110 (Therapeutic exercises 4 units) + 97112 (Neuromuscular reeducation 2 units) + M54.4 (Lumbago with sciatica)

Modifiers: -GP, -59 (distinct services)

Notes: New patient with chronic L4-L5 radiculopathy. High complexity evaluation due to multiple body systems involved. Treatment targets core stability and nerve mobilization. Documentation shows objective measurements and functional outcomes.

Shoulder Impingement Rehabilitation

CPT + ICD-10: 97161 (Evaluation low complexity) + 97110 (Therapeutic exercises 2 units) + 97140 (Manual therapy 1 unit) + 97035 (Ultrasound 1 unit) + M75.40 (Rotator cuff syndrome)

Modifiers: -GP

Notes: Patient with right shoulder impingement. Low complexity evaluation. Treatment includes therapeutic exercises for rotator cuff strengthening, manual therapy, and ultrasound modalities. Goals: pain-free overhead reaching within 4 weeks.
