Dermatology practices perform biopsies, excisions, Mohs micrographic surgery, cryotherapy, and phototherapy — making accurate coding essential for maximizing reimbursement and reducing denials.

📄 Documentation Requirements

  • ✓ Detailed lesion description: location, size, color, borders, and clinical appearance
  • ✓ Anatomic site documentation with precise anatomic location (e.g., left temple, right forearm)
  • ✓ Biopsy type documentation: tangential, punch, or incisional with size and depth
  • ✓ Mohs surgery: stages performed, margins examined, defect size, and reconstruction
  • ✓ Pathology report with histologic diagnosis and margin status
  • ✓ Phototherapy: cumulative dose, number of treatments, and response documentation
  • ✓ Destruction method: cryotherapy, electrosurgery, curettage, or laser
  • ✓ Wound closure documentation: layered closure, complex closure, or secondary intent
  • ✓ Clinical photographs when available for baseline and follow-up comparison
  • ✓ Prior treatment history and failure documentation for repeat procedures

⏱️ Global Surgery Period

Global Period Rules:

0 days for biopsies (11102-11106), lesion destruction (17000-17006), and cryotherapy. 10 days for excisions of benign lesions and Mohs micrographic surgery. 90 days for excision of malignant lesions requiring complex reconstruction. Global period includes pre-op and post-op visits.

💡 Medical Necessity Tips

  • ✓ Document clinical suspicion for malignancy for every biopsy performed
  • ✓ Include lesion size, morphology, and change over time in documentation
  • ✓ For Mohs surgery, document tumor type, location, and prior treatment history
  • ✓ Distinguish between medically necessary and cosmetic procedures clearly
  • ✓ Document prior conservative treatment failure before ordering phototherapy
  • ✓ Include clinical photographs with measurements when available
  • ✓ Reference NCCN guidelines for skin cancer treatment appropriateness
  • ✓ Document family history of skin cancer and sun exposure history when relevant

📝 Billing Guidelines

  • ✓ Bill biopsy (11102, 11104, 11106) based on technique used — not lesion type
  • ✓ Use modifier 59 for separate lesions at different anatomic sites
  • ✓ Bill Mohs stages sequentially (17311, 17312, 17313) — do not skip stages
  • ✓ Include reconstruction codes separately when complex repair performed after Mohs
  • ✓ Ensure pathology specimen processing codes (88305) are not billed by facility and physician
  • ✓ Use appropriate anatomic specificity for ICD-10 codes — distinguish between body sites
  • ✓ Bill phototherapy with cumulative dose documentation for each session
  • ✓ Verify global period for surgical procedures — do not bill post-op visits separately

📊 Example Claim Scenarios

Mohs Surgery for Basal Cell Carcinoma

CPT + ICD-10: 17311 (Mohs first stage, head) + 17312 (second stage) + 12041 (complex closure) + C44.92 (BCC)

Modifiers: None — single lesion, single encounter

Notes: Patient with basal cell carcinoma on right temple. Two stages of Mohs required for clear margins. Complex closure performed. Pathology confirms clear margins.

Biopsy of Suspicious Lesion

CPT + ICD-10: 11104 (Punch biopsy) + L57.0 (Actinic keratosis) or D22.62 (Melanocytic nevi)

Modifiers: -59 if multiple lesions at different sites

Notes: Punch biopsy performed on suspicious pigmented lesion on right forearm. Lesion description and anatomic location documented. Pathology report ordered.

Multiple Actinic Keratosis Destruction

CPT + ICD-10: 17000 (first lesion) + 17003 (10 additional lesions) + L57.0 (Actinic keratosis)

Modifiers: None — single anatomic area

Notes: Cryotherapy performed on 11 actinic keratoses on scalp. First lesion billed with 17000, additional 10 with 17003. Documentation includes lesion count and treatment method.
