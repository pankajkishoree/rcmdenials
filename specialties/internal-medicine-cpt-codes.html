📋 Documentation Requirements

  • ? Physician order or clinical indication for each service billed
  • ? Complete history of present illness (HPI) with onset, location, duration, character, aggravating/relieving factors
  • ? Review of systems (ROS) documented to support E/M level selection
  • ? Physical examination findings with relevant body system documentation
  • ? Medical decision-making complexity documented including diagnosis and treatment options
  • ? Time-based billing documentation when using total time for E/M level selection
  • ? Chronic care management: documented care plan, medication reconciliation, and care coordination activities
  • ? Prolonged service documentation showing total time exceeding face-to-face requirements
  • ? Preventive visit: age-appropriate screening, immunization records, and risk factor counseling
  • ? Progress notes supporting ongoing chronic disease management and treatment plan adjustments

⏱️ Global Surgery Period

Global Period Rules:

0 days for most E/M services and office-based procedures. 10 days for minor procedures performed in the office (e.g., joint injection, skin biopsy). 90 days for major surgical procedures. Post-op visits (99024) are bundled into the global surgical package and should not be billed separately.

✅ Medical Necessity Tips

  • ? Always document the reason for the visit in the HPI  symptoms, complaints, or monitoring
  • ? Match E/M level to documented complexity  medical decision-making or total time
  • ? For chronic care management, document care coordination activities and communication attempts
  • ? Include medication list review and reconciliation in every chronic disease management visit
  • ? Document functional status and impact on daily activities for disability examinations
  • ? For preventive visits, document screening recommendations and patient counseling
  • ? Reference clinical guidelines (ADA, AHA, USPSTF) for appropriate use criteria
  • ? Document shared decision-making for treatment plan changes and medication adjustments

💰 Billing Guidelines

  • ? Use modifier 25 when billing E/M on same day as a procedure  document separately identifiable service
  • ? Bill preventive visit and E/M on same day only when significant, separately identifiable problem exists
  • ? Ensure CCM (99490/99491) is billed once per calendar month per patient with qualifying conditions
  • ? Document total time for prolonged services  minimum 30 minutes for 99354 (office) or 99356 (inpatient)
  • ? Verify which services can be furnished incident-to physician services for higher reimbursement
  • ? Use appropriate place of service codes  POS 11 (office), POS 22 (outpatient hospital)
  • ? Monitor NCCI edits for E/M and procedure bundling pairs
  • ? Ensure CPT codes match documentation  do not upcode based on assumptions

📝 Example Claim Scenarios

Established Diabetic Patient Follow-Up

CPT + ICD-10: 99214 (Established patient, moderate complexity) + E11.65 (T2DM with hyperglycemia)

Modifiers: None required for standalone E/M

Notes: Patient with uncontrolled diabetes returns for medication adjustment. HPI documents elevated blood glucose readings, medication review, and plan adjustment. MDM supports moderate complexity.

Preventive Visit with Problem-Oriented E/M

CPT + ICD-10: 99396 (Established patient, 40-64 years) + 99214 + Z00.00 / I10 (HTN)

Modifiers: -25 on 99214 for separate E/M service

Notes: Annual wellness visit plus separate hypertension management. Documentation clearly distinguishes preventive elements from problem-oriented visit. Modifier 25 appended to E/M code.

Chronic Care Management Service

CPT + ICD-10: 99490 (CCM, 20 minutes) + E11.9 (T2DM) + I10 (HTN)

Modifiers: None required

Notes: Monthly CCM service for patient with diabetes and hypertension. Documentation includes care coordination activities, medication management, and communication with patient between visits.
