Why Track Payer Policy Changes?

Healthcare payer policies change constantly — CMS alone publishes hundreds of LCD and NCD revisions annually, while commercial payers update prior authorization lists, modifier requirements, and reimbursement rules on a rolling basis. Missing a policy change can mean immediate claim denials, compliance risk, and lost revenue.

📋
LCD & NCD Coverage Changes
Local and National Coverage Determinations define which services Medicare will pay for. When an LCD is revised, diagnosis codes, clinical indications, or documentation requirements may change, directly impacting claim approval rates.
💰
Fee Schedule & RVU Updates
CMS publishes annual Medicare Physician Fee Schedule updates that change relative value units (RVUs) and conversion factors. Commercial payers often follow suit, making fee schedule monitoring critical for revenue forecasting.
🔐
Prior Authorization Rule Updates
Prior authorization lists expand frequently — a procedure that didn't require auth last quarter may now require it. Missing new PA requirements is one of the top causes of avoidable claim denials across all specialties.
⚙️
Administrative & Billing Policy Updates
Timely filing windows, modifier usage rules, claims submission requirements, and coordination of benefits policies change without announcement. Our feed surfaces these changes before they cause denial backlogs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What changed in a payer policy and where can I find the details? ▾
Each policy update in our feed includes a dedicated Change Detail View with a side-by-side comparison of the previous and new rule text. Additions are highlighted in green, removals in red. You can also see which specific CPT codes, diagnosis codes, specialties, and provider types are affected by clicking "View Full Details" on any policy card.
When does a payer policy change take effect? ▾
Every policy entry includes an Effective Date section showing the implementation date, any grace period, and whether the change is retroactive. For Medicare LCD/NCD updates, CMS typically provides a 45-90 day implementation window. Commercial payer changes may take effect immediately upon notification. Always verify directly with the payer.
How do payer policy changes affect claim denials and revenue? ▾
Payer policy changes are a leading cause of new denial categories. When a payer tightens prior authorization requirements or revises LCD coverage criteria, previously approved claims may suddenly be denied. Our RCM Intelligence Feed assigns each change a risk level (High/Medium/Low) and provides specific action steps for your billing team to stay ahead of the denial wave.
What types of payer policies does this feed track? ▾
We track: (1) LCD – Local Coverage Determinations from Medicare Administrative Contractors (MACs), (2) NCD – National Coverage Determinations from CMS, (3) Fee Schedule and RVU updates including the annual Medicare Physician Fee Schedule, (4) Prior Authorization rule changes from Medicare and commercial payers, and (5) Administrative & Billing policy updates covering modifiers, timely filing, and claims submission rules.
How accurate and up-to-date is this payer policy information? ▾
Every policy entry in our feed includes source attribution linking to the official CMS, MAC, or commercial payer publication. All entries are timestamped. This feed is updated regularly and is for informational purposes only — it is not a substitute for direct payer verification. We never publish speculative or unverified policy changes.
