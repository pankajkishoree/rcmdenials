Timely Filing Calculator: Never Miss a Deadline Again

Timely filing denials are among the most preventable — and most costly — denials in healthcare billing. When a claim misses the payer's filing deadline, there's no appeal, no exception, and no recovery. Our Timely Filing Calculator computes exact filing deadlines for 30+ insurance payers, so you know exactly when each claim must be submitted.

How the Calculator Works

Select your payer, claim type, and date of service. The tool calculates multiple deadline scenarios: original filing deadline, timely filing limit from denial date, and appeal filing windows. Each deadline is color-coded — green for safe, amber for approaching, and red for expired — so you can instantly assess your filing status.

Why Timely Filing Varies by Payer

Unlike many billing rules, timely filing limits are not standardized across payers. Medicare allows 12 months from date of service for original claims, but commercial payers set their own limits — some as short as 90 days. Medicaid varies by state. The tool accounts for these differences and calculates the correct deadline for each specific payer contract.

Critical Filing Windows

  • Original claim filing — The initial deadline for submitting clean claims
  • Appeal filing deadlines — Windows for appealing denied claims (often shorter than original filing)
  • Redetermination requests — Medicare-specific timelines for first-level appeals
  • Second-level appeals — QIC and ALJ filing windows for complex denials
  • Payer-specific exceptions — Contractual variations that override standard timelines

Who Uses This Tool

AR teams, billing managers, and denial management specialists use this calculator to prioritize claim submissions, identify at-risk claims, and build filing calendars. It's especially valuable for practices managing claims across multiple payers with different filing requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the timely filing deadline for Medicare claims?
Medicare allows 12 months from the date of service for original claims, and 120 days from the date of the denial notice for appeals. However, Medicare Advantage plans may have different timelines. Our calculator provides the exact deadline for your specific claim scenario.
How do timely filing limits differ between payers?
Each payer sets its own timely filing limits. Commercial payers typically range from 90 days to 1 year from date of service. Medicaid varies by state. The tool covers 30+ payers and automatically applies the correct deadline based on your selection.
Can I get an extension on a timely filing deadline?
Some payers allow extensions for good cause exceptions, such as demonstrated system failures or natural disasters. However, these exceptions are rare and require documentation. The tool flags when you're approaching a deadline so you can submit before extensions become necessary.
What happens if I miss a timely filing deadline?
Claims filed after the timely filing limit are automatically denied with a CO-29 (timely filing) or PR-204 (timely filing) denial code. Unlike most denials, timely filing denials cannot be appealed unless you can prove the claim was filed within the deadline. Prevention is the best strategy.
Does the calculator include appeal deadlines?
Yes. The tool calculates both original filing deadlines and appeal filing windows. For denied claims, you'll see the deadline for redetermination requests, second-level appeals, and any other applicable appeal stages based on the denial date.
